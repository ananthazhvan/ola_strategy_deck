# Open Design — Ola Electric Strategy Deck

## Project Intent & Ideology

### The Core Idea

This is not a standard McKinsey-style strategy deck. This is a **neural knowledge graph presentation** — a visual, interconnected system where every slide is a node in a larger network, and every connection tells a story.

The presentation is built around the concept that **Ola Electric's problems are not isolated** — they cascade. The service crisis caused the trust collapse which caused the market share decline which forced the ASP drop which triggered the margin paradox which exposed the Gigafactory utilization risk. A linear slide deck can't show that. A knowledge graph can.

### Why This Approach

**2,613 teams are registered for this competition.** 2,500 of them will submit standard decks: blue headers, bullet points, bar charts, stock photos of scooters. This deck will be the one judges remember.

The graph concept signals something powerful: *"This team didn't just research Ola — they mapped the entire ecosystem and understand how every problem connects."* That's exactly what a strategy competition wants to see.

The evaluators are young finance students at IIT BHU who are tired of regular McKinsey-style presentations. They want something new. This delivers that.

### What I Genuinely Want

1. **A presentation that shows systems thinking** — not just "here's a problem, here's a solution" but "here's how everything connects, and here's the leverage point"
2. **Visual proof of depth** — the graph shows at a glance that 112+ files of research went into this, not just a surface-level analysis
3. **A narrative that flows through connections** — each slide zooms into one node, but the connections to other nodes are always visible, creating a sense of a living, breathing analysis
4. **Something that looks like it took real effort** — because it did. This isn't a template filled with bullet points. This is a custom-built visual system.
5. **A deck that works as a PDF for Round 1** (static submission) but has the potential for an interactive HTML version for Round 2 if needed

## The Neural Architecture Concept

### How It Works

Each slide has:
- **One central node** (the main topic of that slide)
- **4-6 connected nodes** radiating outward (the topics directly related to it)
- **3 key takeaways** positioned around the graph (the actionable insights with supporting data)
- **The full knowledge graph** appears as an "end credits" page at the end, showing the entire 112-node network

The visual metaphor is: **you're looking at a neural network, and each slide is you focusing on one neuron and seeing what it's connected to.**

### The Obsidian Theme

The design is inspired by Obsidian's knowledge graph visualization:
- **Dark background** (#0a0e17 or similar deep dark)
- **Colored nodes** by category (Companies, Financial, Market, Risks, Solutions, Strategy, Technology, Opportunities)
- **Connecting lines** showing relationships between nodes
- **Glassmorphism panels** for the content areas (frosted glass effect)
- **Clean typography** (Inter or similar sans-serif)

This isn't just aesthetic — it reinforces the core message: Ola's situation is a complex system, and you need a systems-thinking approach to solve it.

## The Slide Architecture

### Cover Slide
- Title: "Ola Electric: From Crisis to Category King"
- Subtitle: "Repositioning India's Most Ambitious EV Brand"

- Visual: A focused sub-graph showing "Ola Electric" as the central node with its key connections

### Slide 1: The Market — Growing 6.9x While Ola Shrinks 52%
- **Central node:** E2W Market India
- **Connected nodes:** VAHAN Market Share Story, Subsidy Cliff Jul 2026, Chinese OEM Export Competition, Legacy OEM Structural Advantage, Mass Market E2W
- **3 Takeaways:**
  1. E2W market grew 6.9x (21,500 → 148,713 units/month) while Ola's share collapsed from 55.81% to 15.95%
  2. PM E-DRIVE subsidy ends Jul 31, 2026 — ₹5,000 = 123% of Ola's per-unit EBITDA profit
  3. Legacy players (TVS+Bajaj+Hero) now control 55% EV share — a complete reversal in 24 months
- **Data needed:** VAHAN registration data, market share percentages, subsidy amounts, competitor unit sales

### Slide 2: The Trust Collapse — CCPA as the Inflection Point
- **Central node:** Brand Trust Deficit
- **Connected nodes:** CCPA Regulatory Intervention, Customer Sentiment Analysis, Service Center Crisis, Consumer Court Crisis, Google Play Store Rating, Social Media Sentiment
- **3 Takeaways:**
  1. 10,000+ CCPA complaints made Ola a Top 5 most-complained-about company across ALL sectors in India
  2. Only 300 of 935 centers had repair capability (32%) — parts availability 60-75% vs 95% industry standard
  3. CCPA notice (Oct 2024) was the inflection point: share fell from 26.40% to 9.71% in 12 months
- **Data needed:** CCPA complaint numbers, service center stats, parts availability percentages, market share before/after CCPA

### Slide 3: The Margin Paradox — Ola's Counterintuitive Strength
- **Central node:** Margin Paradox
- **Connected nodes:** Gigafactory Updates, Gen 2 Platform, Operating Leverage, Product Mix Cannibalization, Quarterly Losses, Unit Economics, Post-IPO Quarterly Calls
- **3 Takeaways:**
  1. ASP fell 34% (₹1.35L → ₹89K) but gross profit/unit **rose 26%** (₹24,300 → ₹30,527)
  2. Battery cost: $130 → $80/kWh (38% reduction) via Gigafactory — the single biggest margin lever
  3. EBITDA breakeven dropped from ~40K to ~15K units/month — Ola is within striking distance of cash-flow positivity
- **Data needed:** ASP trajectory, gross profit per unit, battery cost per kWh, EBITDA margins by quarter, breakeven unit numbers

### Slide 4: Competitive Firepower — The Pincer Trap
- **Central node:** Competitor Firepower
- **Connected nodes:** Bajaj Auto, TVS Motor, Hero MotoCorp, Ather Energy, Competition, VAHAN Market Share Story, Subsidy Cliff Jul 2026, Margin Paradox
- **3 Takeaways:**
  1. Bajaj: ₹38,552 Cr reserves (24x Ola's cash), Chetak already profitable, Rs 400/visit service cost
  2. Hero: 6,000+ dealers, 206% YoY growth, 5-year warranty — fastest scale-up trajectory
  3. TVS: 23% share leader, 371K units, 1,000+ dealers, 95% parts availability
- **Data needed:** Competitor market caps, reserves, dealer counts, service costs, unit sales, warranty terms, EBITDA margins

### Slide 5: The Gigafactory — Asset or Stranded Risk?
- **Central node:** Gigafactory Updates
- **Connected nodes:** 4680 Cell Tech, LFP 46100 Cell, Global Cell Price Parity, Battery Supply Chain, Backward Integration, Gigafactory Execution, B2B Battery Pivot, Cell Manufacturing
- **3 Takeaways:**
  1. 6 GWh operational, 16% utilization, ₹4,000 Cr invested — massive excess capacity
  2. LFP 46100 at $80/kWh = import parity (NOT advantage) — make-vs-buy is neutral today
  3. Becomes advantageous at Phase 2 (12 GWh, $75/kWh, Jul 2027) — but B2B cell sales face 900 GWh global glut
- **Data needed:** Gigafactory capacity timeline, cell production numbers, battery cost trajectory, global cell capacity/overcapacity data, PLI scheme details

### Slide 6: GTM — Project Horizon (4 Phases)
- **Central node:** GTM Strategy Project Horizon
- **Connected nodes:** Decentralized Service Network, Marketing Campaign Radical Transparency, Zero-Tolerance QA, Captive Financing and Affordability, Battery-First Strategic Pivot, Subsidy Cliff Jul 2026, VAHAN Market Share Story, Motorcycle Market, Export Markets, Roadster, S1 X, Gen 3 Platform
- **3 Takeaways:**
  1. Phase 1 (May-Oct 2026): Survive subsidy cliff, deploy Hub & Spoke (300 hubs + 2,000 garages), target EBITDA ≥+2%
  2. Phase 2 (Nov 2026-Apr 2027): Gen 3 "Reliable Relaunch," captive financing live, target share ≥18%, NPS neutral
  3. Phase 3-4 (May 2027-Oct 2028): Roadster motorcycle entry + Africa/SEA exports, target ₹500 Cr export revenue
- **Data needed:** Phase timelines, investment amounts, success metrics, target market shares, export TAM estimates

### Slide 7: Service Recovery — Hub & Spoke Model
- **Central node:** Decentralized Service Network
- **Connected nodes:** Service Center Crisis, Customer Experience Gap, D2C Omnichannel, Bajaj Auto, TVS Motor, Hero MotoCorp, Brand Trust Deficit, CCPA Regulatory Intervention, Consumer Court Crisis, GTM Strategy Project Horizon, Marketing Campaign Radical Transparency, Zero-Tolerance QA, Service Recovery Initiatives
- **3 Takeaways:**
  1. 300 Ola hubs (complex repairs) + 2,000 certified local garages (routine service) = 2,300 service points
  2. Cost: ₹300 Cr total (₹60L/hub + ₹60K/garage) vs ₹1.5 Cr per Ola-owned center — 10x more efficient
  3. Target: 95% parts availability, 1-2 day turnaround, 95% of customers within 15km of service
- **Data needed:** Current service metrics, competitor service benchmarks, cost per service point, parts availability percentages, turnaround times

### Slide 8: Marketing — Radical Transparency (₹80 Cr)
- **Central node:** Marketing Campaign Radical Transparency
- **Connected nodes:** Brand Trust Deficit, Brand Perception, Customer Sentiment Analysis, CCPA Regulatory Intervention, Consumer Court Crisis, Social Media Sentiment, Google Play Store Rating, Net Promoter Score, Bhavish Aggarwal, Gigafactory Updates, Decentralized Service Network, Zero-Tolerance QA, Investor Confidence Restoration, Service Recovery Initiatives
- **3 Takeaways:**
  1. Live Gigafactory streams + public service dashboard + engineers not influencers — truth becomes the differentiator
  2. 48-hour recall promise with loaner + ₹5,000 compensation — preempts consumer court crisis
  3. Channel strategy: 35% YouTube, 25% owned platforms, 20% earned media, 0% traditional/OOH, 0% performance ads
- **Data needed:** Current brand perception metrics, NPS scores, app ratings, campaign budget allocation, success metric targets

### Slide 9: Post-Subsidy Survival — The 3-Part Architecture
- **Central node:** Post-Subsidy Pricing Architecture
- **Connected nodes:** Subsidy Cliff Jul 2026, PM E-DRIVE Subsidy, FAME II Scheme, Gigafactory Updates, LFP 46100 Cell, Margin Paradox, Operating Leverage, Unit Economics, S1 X, State EV Policies, PLI Dual Beneficiary, Captive Financing and Affordability, GTM Strategy Project Horizon, Investor Confidence Restoration
- **3 Takeaways:**
  1. Cost hardening: ₹3,000/unit saved (Gigafactory cell cost, Gen 3 BOM, logistics, PLI disbursement)
  2. State arbitrage: ₹1,500/unit recovered (shift sales to TN, Delhi, Maharashtra — high-subsidy states)
  3. Product mix: ₹500/unit absorbed (Ola Care subscription, accessories) — S1 X holds at ₹69,999, premium models absorb pass-through
- **Data needed:** Subsidy amounts by state, cost reduction breakdown, product pricing, EBITDA impact scenarios

### Slide 10: The Ask — 12-Month Execution Checklist
- **Central node:** The Ola Turnaround Strategy
- **Connected to:** EVERYTHING — this is the synthesis slide. The central node connects to all major solution nodes and key risk nodes.
- **3 Takeaways:**
  1. Survive Jul 2026 cliff → deploy Hub & Spoke → launch Gen 3 reliability campaign → start captive financing → prepare Roadster
  2. Ola doesn't need to beat TVS/Bajaj at their game — it needs to survive 12 months, prove Gen 3 reliability, and enter the motorcycle greenfield (10.8M units, <1% EV)
  3. The battery pivot changes the valuation story: from "loss-making scooter startup" at ₹15,923 Cr to "energy infrastructure company" at ₹29,400 Cr implied (₹67/share, 85-130% upside)
- **Data needed:** 12-month timeline, key milestones, valuation comparison, motorcycle market size, investment thesis summary

## Design Principles for the Agent

1. **Dark theme throughout** — deep dark background (#0a0e17 or similar), light text
2. **Graph-centric layout** — each slide's visual anchor is the node graph, not text
3. **Minimal text, maximum impact** — 3 takeaways per slide, each with a data point. No walls of text.
4. **Consistent node styling** — same color scheme, same node sizes, same connection line styles across all slides
5. **Glassmorphism for content panels** — frosted glass effect for the takeaway text areas
6. **Professional but bold** — this isn't a corporate deck. It's a strategy competition entry. It should look confident and different.
7. **Data accuracy is paramount** — every number must come from the vault. No hallucinated metrics.
8. **The graph tells the story** — the connections between nodes should be meaningful, not decorative. If two nodes are connected, there's a real relationship.

## Final Notes for the Agent

- **Do not hallucinate data.** If a number isn't in the vault, don't invent it. Use "—" or note that data is unavailable.
- **The graph is the hero.** Text supports the graph, not the other way around.
- **Each slide should feel like a "zoom in" on one part of the larger system.** The viewer should always sense that there's more context beyond what's on the current slide.
- **The end credits slide is the payoff.** After seeing 10 focused slides, the viewer sees the full 112-node network and understands the depth of analysis.
- **This is a strategy competition, not a design competition.** The design should be impressive, but the strategic insight is what wins. Every visual choice should serve the strategy narrative.
- **PDF export must work.** Round 1 submission is a static PDF. Test that all graphs, text, and styling render correctly in PDF format.

This presentation is built on a simple belief: **the best strategy presentations don't just tell you what to do — they show you how everything connects.**

Ola Electric's situation is complex. The service crisis, the trust collapse, the market share decline, the margin paradox, the Gigafactory utilization risk, the subsidy cliff, the competitive pincer — these aren't separate problems. They're one interconnected system.

The knowledge graph format is the only way to honestly represent that complexity while still delivering clear, actionable recommendations.

That's what wins strategy competitions.
