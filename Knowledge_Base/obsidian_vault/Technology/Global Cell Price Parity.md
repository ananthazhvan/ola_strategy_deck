# Global Cell Price Parity

The tension between Ola Electric's Gigafactory investment and rapidly declining global cell prices is one of the most consequential strategic questions facing the company. Global LFP battery pack prices have fallen 43% in four years -- from $140/kWh (2022) to $80/kWh (2026) -- driven by a massive buildout of Chinese manufacturing capacity that has created approximately 900 GWh of excess supply in 2026 alone. This Chinese-driven overcapacity means any cell manufacturer, including Ola's Gigafactory, must compete against a global price floor set by the lowest-cost Chinese producers.

Ola's current position is precarious. At $85/kWh for its in-house NMC+LFP pilot production (Q4 FY26), Ola is approximately 6% above the global LFP pack price of $80/kWh. At the LFP 46100 cell level, announced at $80/kWh (Apr 2026), Ola achieves cost parity but not a cost advantage. The Gigafactory's economic thesis only fully validates at the Phase 2 target of $75/kWh (12 GWh, Jul 2027), which would give Ola a ~6% cost advantage over global prices, and the full build-out target of $70/kWh (Mar 2028) for a ~12.5% advantage.

The fundamental challenge is scale. Ola's own vehicle sales of 164,000 units in FY26 can only absorb a fraction of the 6 GWh capacity already built. B2B cell supply talks (May 2026) represent a strategic recognition that the Gigafactory must sell cells externally to achieve the scale required for cost-competitive production. ## Key Data Points

- Global LFP pack price decline: $140/kWh (2022) → $80/kWh (2026) -- 43% drop
- Global NMC pack price: $160/kWh (2022) → $100/kWh (2026) -- 37.5% drop
- Global cell manufacturing capacity: 1,500 GWh (2022) → 4,200 GWh (2026) -- 180% expansion
- Excess global cell supply in 2026: ~900 GWh
- Ola in-house LFP 46100 cell cost: $80/kWh (Apr 2026) -- at global parity
- Ola Phase 2 target: $75/kWh at 12 GWh (Jul 2027) -- below global price
- Ola full build-out target: $70/kWh at 20 GWh (Mar 2028) -- 12.5% below 2026 global prices
- Ola FY26 vehicle sales: 164,000 units -- insufficient to absorb 6 GWh capacity
- Separator film price decline: $2.5/sqm → $1.7/sqm (-32%); Electrolyte: $12/kg → $5.2/kg (-57%)
- Lithium carbonate V-shaped recovery: CNY 95,000 (2025 H1) → CNY 192,000 (2026 Current) -- 102% increase
