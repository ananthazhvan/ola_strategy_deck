# Battery Innovation Centre

The Battery Innovation Centre (BIC) is Ola Electric's dedicated R&D facility for cell chemistry development, battery pack engineering, and manufacturing process innovation, located within the Tamil Nadu EV Hub near the Krishnagiri Gigafactory. The BIC represents Ola's bet that vertical integration into cell development -- rather than procurement from Chinese suppliers -- will deliver a durable cost advantage in the Indian E2W market.

Ola's R&D spend reached Rs 5,077 Mn (18.25% of total income) in FY23, a significant portion directed at cell technology. The BIC's most critical achievement is the 35% battery cost reduction from $130/kWh (imported NMC cells, Q1 FY25) to $85/kWh (in-house NMC 4680 + LFP 46100 pilot, Q4 FY26). Since battery constitutes ~35% of BOM for the S1 Pro, every dollar reduction in per-kWh cell cost translates directly into gross margin improvement -- demonstrated by Ola's margin expansion from 12% (Q4 FY25) to 36% (Q4 FY26).

The BIC's strategic pivot from NMC 4680 to LFP 46100 chemistry, announced April 2026, was validated by external commodity markets: cobalt prices surged 133% (from $24,000/MT to $56,000/MT) due to DRC supply constraints, making NMC cells structurally more expensive. LFP's advantages -- 30-40% cheaper raw materials, better thermal stability for Indian summers (45C+), and 3,000+ cycle life vs 1,500 for NMC -- aligned perfectly with the Indian mass market use case. The BIC's ongoing work targets $70/kWh at 20 GWh full build-out by March 2028, which would undercut global LFP pack prices.

## Key Data Points

- R&D spend FY23: Rs 5,077 Mn (18.25% of total income); declined as % of revenue as commercial scale grew
- Battery cost trajectory: $130/kWh (imported) → $110/kWh (in-house NMC) → $85/kWh (NMC+LFP pilot) → $70/kWh target (full build-out)
- Battery as % of BOM for S1 Pro: ~35% as of Sep 2023
- Cobalt price surge: $24,000/MT (2025 H2) → $56,000/MT (2026 Current) -- 133% increase, validating LFP pivot
- Cell PLI allocation: 20 GWh -- the most by any recipient, with milestones of 1/5/10/20 GWh in years 1-4
- Gigafactory progress: 0 GWh (Mar 2024) → 5.9 GWh (Mar 2026) → 6.0 GWh LFP pilot (Apr 2026)
- LFP advantages: 30-40% cheaper raw materials, no cobalt/nickel, 3,000+ cycles, thermal stability at 45C+
