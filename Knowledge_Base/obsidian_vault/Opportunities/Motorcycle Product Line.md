# Motorcycle Product Line

Ola Electric has announced four electric motorcycle models that extend the company's addressable market beyond scooters: the Diamondhead (performance-oriented), Adventure (touring/adventure), Roadster (commuter), and Cruiser (lifestyle/cruiser). The Roadster entered commercial production in FY26, accounting for 2% of Ola's product mix by Q4 FY26, and represents the company's first foray beyond the S1 scooter platform.

The motorcycle line addresses two strategic imperatives. First, it expands Ola's total addressable market into the motorcycle segment, which accounts for approximately 70% of India's domestic 2W market (vs ~30% for scooters). Second, it creates a product portfolio capable of addressing export markets where motorcycles (not scooters) dominate -- particularly Africa, where commercial delivery use cases demand heavy-duty motorcycle platforms.

However, a critical product gap persists. Export market analysis identifies Nigeria (200K units, 40% CAGR) and other African markets as requiring a heavy-duty motorcycle with swappable battery architecture for commercial delivery use. None of the four announced models explicitly targets this commercial-grade segment, and none incorporates the swappable battery architecture that African markets demand for Battery-as-a-Service models. Competitors including Spiro, MAX, and Ampersand already offer such products in African markets.

## Key Data Points

- Four announced models: Diamondhead, Adventure, Roadster, Cruiser
- Roadster entered production in FY26 at 2% of Ola's quarterly product mix
- Ola's 5-model scooter lineup (S1 range) covers Rs 69,999 to Rs 199,000 -- the widest price band in the industry
- Motorcycles represent ~70% of India's domestic 2W market vs ~30% for scooters
- Nigeria (Africa's largest addressable market) requires heavy-duty motorcycle with swappable battery
- Competitors Spiro, MAX, and Ampersand already offer commercial motorcycles in Africa
- Ola's Ola Fleet Technologies entity previously operated the experience centre network that would sell these motorcycles
