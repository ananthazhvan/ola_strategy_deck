# Ola Shakti / Energy Business Pivot

Ola Electric announced its residential and commercial energy storage brand, Ola Shakti, as a strategic diversification from pure scooter manufacturing into the broader energy business. The pivot reflects a fundamental reality: the Gigafactory's 6 GWh capacity was sized for an optimistic demand scenario where Ola sold 1M+ scooters annually, but actual FY26 sales of 164,000 units consume barely 0.5 GWh of capacity. The remaining 5.5 GWh must find external buyers or become a stranded asset bleeding depreciation (Rs 566 Cr/year) and interest costs (Rs 366 Cr/year).

The B2B pivot has two channels. First, Ola Shakti targets stationary energy storage for residential, commercial, and industrial applications, leveraging LFP chemistry's thermal stability and long cycle life (3,000+ cycles vs 1,500 for NMC). India's growing energy storage market, driven by renewable integration targets and unreliable grid infrastructure, offers a natural adjacency. Second, Ola is exploring cell supply to other Indian OEMs in the E2W, E3W, and light EV segments, pitching domestic supply chain security against reliance on Chinese imports. Management's stated Q4 FY26 focus was explicitly "B2B Battery Supply & Margin Defense."

However, the B2B strategy faces a formidable headwind. The global cell manufacturing capacity reached 4,200 GWh in 2026 with ~900 GWh of excess supply, driving LFP pack prices to $80/kWh — exactly the cost Ola targets for its LFP 46100 cells. As the battery supply chain data explicitly notes: the global overcapacity is "good for Ola if buying; bad if selling B2B." Chinese manufacturers (CATL, BYD) have decades of scale advantage, established global distribution, and the ability to sustain pricing at or below cost for market share. Ola's $80/kWh cost offers zero price advantage, meaning it must compete on delivery reliability, domestic content preference, and relationship — all unproven for a company that has never sold a cell externally.

## Key Data Points

- Gigafactory capacity: 6.0 GWh (Apr-2026), targeting 12 GWh (Jul-2027), 20 GWh (Mar-2028)
- Internal consumption: ~0.4-0.6 GWh/year at current 164K unit run-rate
- Estimated utilization: ~16% — 84% of capacity idle
- Management focus shifted to "B2B Battery Supply & Margin Defense" in Q4 FY26
- Global LFP pack price: $80/kWh (down 43% from $140/kWh peak in 2022)
- Ola's LFP 46100 cost: $80/kWh at Apr-2026 — at parity, no advantage
- Global cell overcapacity: ~900 GWh (21% of total capacity)
- Zero confirmed B2B orders or revenue as of May 2026
- Ola's Revenue Per Unit rose from Rs 151,818 (FY24) to Rs 223,902 (FY26) despite ASP decline — suggesting nascent non-vehicle revenue, though no B2B revenue line exists in financial statements
- Ola qualifies for both PLI schemes (Auto PLI + ACC Cell PLI), a dual benefit unique among Indian EV players
