> [!insight] The Single Most Important Thing
> The 417.59-acre SIPCOT-leased EV Hub in Krishnagiri concentrates Ola's entire manufacturing, R&D, and supplier ecosystem in one geography — creating powerful cost synergies via co-location but also exposing the entire company to single-location execution risk.

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|

## Hub Components

1. **Ola Futurefactory** — E2W manufacturing, 1M units/year installed capacity
2. **Ola Gigafactory** — Cell manufacturing (under construction)
3. **Co-located Supplier Park** — Integrated supply chain within the hub
4. **R&D / BIC** — Battery Innovation Centre for cell technology development

| Incentive | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| SGST Reimbursement | 100% for 20 years on EVs sold + registered in TN |
| Investment Promotion Subsidy | Capital assistance |
| GST on Capital Goods | Incentive in inverted tax situations |
| Land Cost Subsidy | Concessional SIPCOT lease rates |
| Electricity Tax Incentive | Reduced power cost for manufacturing |
| Corporate Tax Rate | 15% for new manufacturing companies |
| Recognized in FY23 | ₹204.6 Cr capital + revenue assistance + ₹69.2 Cr interest income on govt grant |

## Strategic Importance

1. **Integrated ecosystem** — Manufacturing, supply chain, and R&D co-located
2. **Cost advantages** — State incentives + logistics savings from co-located suppliers
3. **Single location risk** — All manufacturing concentrated in one geography
4. **MOU obligations** — Must meet minimum investment and employment targets or face penalties/lease cancellation
