> [!insight] The Single Most Important Thing
> S1 X cannibalization is a red herring — gross profit per unit actually INCREASED 26% (₹24,300 → ₹30,527) despite ASP falling 34%, because [[Technology/Gigafactory_Updates|Gigafactory cells]] and the simpler S1 X BOM collapsed COGS faster than the selling price declined.

> [!data] Source: PRODUCT_MIX_QUARTERLY.csv
> S1 X grew from 15% of sales (Q1 FY25) to 75% (Q3 FY26), while blended ASP fell from ₹1.35L to ₹0.89L. Yet gross margin improved from 18% to 34.3% — a 16.3pp expansion.

> [!data] Source: GIGAFACTORY_MILESTONES.csv
> Battery cost per kWh fell from $130 (imported, FY24) to $80 (in-house [[Technology/4680 Cell Tech|LFP 46100]], Apr 2026) — a 38% reduction. BOM reduction reached 30%, enabling the margin improvement.

> [!data] Source: EV_SEGMENT_COMPARISON.csv
> At blended ASP ₹89,000, Ola has the lowest revenue/unit among peers (TVS ₹126,686, Bajaj ₹138,408, Ather ₹139,582) yet achieved positive [[Strategy/Operating Leverage|EBITDA]] — a first in the industry for a mass-market E2W.

> [!data] Source: QUARTERLY_FINANCIALS_EXT.csv
> EBITDA breakeven dropped from ~40,000 to ~15,000 units/month. At 35K units (Q3 FY26), Ola posted +2.1% EBITDA — the first positive quarter — proving the [[Strategy/Backward Integration|restructuring thesis]].

## The Cannibalization Paradox

### What Looks Bad (Revenue Side)
- ASP dropped 34%: from ₹1.35L (Q1 FY25) to ₹0.89L (Q3 FY26)
- Revenue per unit crashed, making the top-line shrink faster than volume
- S1 X at ₹69,999-89,999 competes with ICE scooters, dragging down premium perception

### What's Actually Good (Margin Side)
- S1 X has a **simpler BOM** — fewer components, cheaper assembly
- Gen 3 platform reduced manufacturing complexity
- Gigafactory LFP cells cut battery costs by ~30%
- **Result: 34.3% gross margin on lower ASP** (better than 18% margin on higher ASP)

### The Math
| Scenario | ASP (₹) | Gross Margin (%) | Gross Profit per Unit (₹) |
|----------|---------|------------------|--------------------------|
| Q1 FY25 (S1 Pro heavy) | 1,35,000 | ~18% | ~24,300 |
| Q3 FY26 (S1 X heavy + Gigafactory) | 89,000 | ~34.3% | ~30,527 |

**Gross profit per unit actually INCREASED by 26%** despite ASP falling 34%. The S1 X is not killing margins — it's improving them, but only because the Gigafactory slashed input costs.

### Strategic Implication
The S1 X "cannibalization" is a red herring. The real problem is:
1. **Trust deficit** → fewer total buyers (164K vs 359K)
2. **NOT** bad unit economics → margins are actually record-high

If Ola can rebuild trust and get back to 30,000+ units/month at current S1 X mix + Gigafactory costs, they would be solidly EBITDA-positive.
