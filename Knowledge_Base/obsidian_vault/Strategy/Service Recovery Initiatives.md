# Service Recovery Initiatives

Ola Electric's service crisis — the single largest driver of its market share collapse from 47% to 8.6% — triggered a multi-pronged operational response beginning in late 2024. Four named initiatives were deployed in sequence, each targeting a different dimension of the service failure: network footprint, field service reach, delivery speed, and cost structure. Together they represent Ola's attempt to fix a broken D2C service model that had only ~300 repair-capable centers serving over 1M vehicles on road.

## Project Vistaar (Network Transformation, Nov 2024)

Launched when Ola's market share had already fallen to ~24%, Project Vistaar was the first structural response to the service crisis. It aimed to optimize the retail and service footprint by converting pure-sales experience centers into combined sales-and-service outlets. At launch, only ~300 of 900 experience centers had repair capability, meaning 67% were showrooms with no service function. Vistaar was designed to close this gap, reducing average delivery time from 12 days to 3-4 days at select locations. However, the initiative was reactive — it came after the CCPA show-cause notice (Oct 2024) had already triggered mass consumer defection, and the structural constraint of centralized parts supply was not addressed.

## Hyperservice (Q1 FY26)

The most aggressive service recovery measure, Hyperservice deployed 1,000 mobile service vans across India, equipped with AI diagnostics for remote troubleshooting. The initiative claimed to reduce the service backlog by 50% by Q3 FY26 and achieve 80% same-day resolution — a dramatic improvement from the 7-15 day turnaround at the crisis peak. AI diagnostics enabled technicians to identify issues remotely before dispatching the correct parts, reducing repeat visits. Operational data showed service turnaround time improved from 7-15 days (peak) to 3-4 days (post-Hyperservice), though this remains 2-3x worse than the 1-2 day industry standard maintained by Bajaj (Rs 400/visit), TVS, and Hero.

## HyperDelivery (Same-Day Delivery)

HyperDelivery introduced same-day delivery capability at select experience centers, aiming to improve the purchase experience. In an environment where Ola's primary competitive weakness was post-purchase service, faster delivery was a lever to improve the initial customer experience. The initiative was operationally sound but strategically limited — faster delivery does not address the trust deficit that discourages customers from making the purchase decision in the first place.

## Project Lakshya (Cost Optimization)

Project Lakshya targeted the cost side of the crisis: OpEx was slashed from Rs 840 Cr (Q4 FY25 peak) to Rs 484 Cr (Q3 FY26) — a 42% reduction in two quarters. Thousands of sales staff were let go, underperforming experience centers were closed, and marketing spend was cut drastically. This restructuring lowered the EBITDA breakeven threshold from ~40,000 units/month to ~15,000 units/month. While essential for survival, the cost cuts reduced Ola's sales capacity exactly when it needed to rebuild volume, creating a tension between financial survival and growth recovery.

## Effectiveness Assessment

The recovery initiatives show mixed results. Operationally, service metrics improved: backlog reduced, turnaround times compressed, and market share stabilized at ~16% (Q4 FY26) from the 8.6% trough (Oct 2025). Financially, the combined effect of cost restructuring and gross margin improvement (12% to 36%) drove EBITDA positive for the first time (+2.1% in Q3 FY26, +4.5% in Q4 FY26). However, commercially, sales have not recovered to sustainable levels: Q4 FY26 monthly average of ~13,000 units remains below the 15,000 breakeven threshold, and the 39K quarterly units is a fraction of the 108K peak.

The core issue remains structural: parts availability at 60-75% (vs 95% industry standard) and only ~300 repair-capable service points cannot be fully addressed by mobile vans and AI diagnostics. Legacy players with 1,000+ full-service points and 30-40 year parts supply chains maintain an infrastructure advantage that operational initiatives cannot quickly close.

## Key Data Points

- Project Vistaar: launched Nov 2024 at ~24% market share, targeted network transformation
- Hyperservice: 1,000 mobile service vans deployed Q1 FY26
- AI diagnostics enabled remote troubleshooting and parts pre-dispatch
- Claimed 50% backlog reduction by Q3 FY26 (self-reported, unverified)
- Claimed 80% same-day resolution (self-reported, unverified)
- Turnaround time improved from 7-15 days (peak) to 3-4 days (post-Hyperservice)
- Industry standard turnaround: 1-2 days (Bajaj, TVS, Hero)
- Parts availability: 60-75% (Ola) vs 95% (industry standard)
- Project Lakshya: OpEx reduced from Rs 840 Cr to Rs 484 Cr (-42%)
- EBITDA breakeven lowered from ~40K to ~15K units/month
- App rating remained at 3.2/5.0 despite operational improvements
- Market share recovered from 8.6% (Oct 2025 trough) to 15.95% (Mar 2026)
- Q4 FY26 sales: 39K units vs 108K peak quarterly volume
