# Operating Leverage Gains

---

## The Trend

| Metric | FY22 | FY23 | Q1 FY24 |
|--------|------|------|---------|
| Revenue (₹ Cr) | 373 | 2,631 | 1,243 |
| Employee Cost / Revenue | 61.9% | 15.3% | 7.4% |
| Ad Spend / Revenue | 10.8% | — | 1.0% |
| R&D Spend / Revenue | 38.5% | 18.3% | 7.3% |
| Asset Turnover | 0.34 | — | 2.68 (ann.) |
| Adj. Gross Margin | -5.4% | 7.6% | 13.2% |

---

## What's Driving It

### 1. Fixed Cost Absorption
As revenue scaled from ₹373 Cr → ₹5,010 Cr, fixed costs (factory, corporate, R&D infrastructure) were spread across more units.

### 2. Gen 2 Platform Efficiency
[[Technology/Gen 2 Platform|68.85% component commonality]] reduces:
- Manufacturing complexity
- Inventory requirements
- Supplier management overhead
- Per-unit R&D amortization

### 3. In-House Manufacturing
- Battery packs, motors, frames made in-house
- Eliminates supplier margins on key components
- Started motor + frame manufacturing Aug 2023

### 4. D2C Efficiency
- [[Strategy/D2C Omnichannel|Brought experience centres in-house]] (Jul 2023)
- Eliminated ₹700 Cr+ annual fee to Ola Fleet Technologies
- Digital-first model reduces per-sale distribution cost

---

## The Problem

Operating leverage works in **both directions**. With [[Risks/Revenue Decline FY25|FY25 revenue declining 9.9%]], fixed costs become a burden again:
- Factory under-utilization (1M capacity, lower volumes)
- R&D spend must continue regardless of sales
- Experience centre costs are largely fixed

> Ola's selling and admin expenses ballooned from Rs 58 Cr (FY21) to Rs 1,598 Cr (FY25) — a 27.5x increase in 4 years — while sales grew from Rs 0.86 Cr to Rs 4,514 Cr. The SG&A-to-sales ratio deteriorated from 67:1 (FY21) to 35% of revenue (FY25). This cost structure was built for the 50%+ market share era and became unsustainable when share collapsed.
> Causal chain: High-growth spending→fixed cost build-up→volume collapse→SG&A unsustainable→restructuring required→breakeven lowered→extreme cost cuts implemented
