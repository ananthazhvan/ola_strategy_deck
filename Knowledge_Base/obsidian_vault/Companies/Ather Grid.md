# Ather Grid

The Ather Grid is Ather Energy's proprietary network of public electric two-wheeler charging points. With 2,000+ charging points across India, it is the most extensive purpose-built E2W charging infrastructure in the country — significantly larger than any comparable network from Ola, TVS, Bajaj, or Hero. The Grid is free for Ather owners, creating a direct economic incentive for ownership continuity: the cost of switching to a non-Ather EV includes losing access to free charging.

## Strategic Importance as a Competitive Moat

The Ather Grid serves three distinct competitive functions simultaneously. First, it addresses the single largest barrier to EV adoption — range anxiety — by providing public charging infrastructure that is reliable and accessible. Second, it creates a switching cost for Ather owners: once a customer experiences free charging convenience, switching to a competitor means either paying for charging or relying on home charging alone. Third, it signals long-term commitment to the EV ecosystem, reinforcing Ather's premium brand positioning as the quality-focused, infrastructure-backed alternative to Ola.

Ather is investing aggressively to widen this moat. The Rs 2,497 Cr raised in FY26 is being deployed partly to expand the Grid beyond its current 2,000+ points. Ather's total assets surged from Rs 2,101 Cr to Rs 4,722 Cr in a single year, reflecting this infrastructure build-out. Meanwhile, Ola — which operates no comparable public charging network — must rely on home charging and third-party CPO (Charge Point Operator) networks, losing the ecosystem lock-in that Ather Grid provides.

## Competitive Landscape in Charging Infrastructure

Hero MotoCorp is deploying 3,600+ fast-charging stations, making it the only competitor with a larger absolute charging infrastructure commitment than Ather Grid. However, Hero's network is being deployed through its 6,000+ dealer network, not as a unified proprietary network. Ather Grid's advantage is that it is a single, branded, quality-controlled network with consistent user experience — analogous to Tesla's Supercharger strategy. The 700 experience centers also serve as charging points, creating a combined service+charging footprint that Ola, with only ~300 repair-capable centers and no charging network, cannot match.

## Financial Impact on Ather's Model

The Ather Grid is not yet a direct profit center (charging is free for Ather owners), but it creates indirect value through: (1) higher customer retention rates, (2) ability to maintain premium pricing (Rs 139,582/unit ASP vs industry average), (3) data collection on usage patterns that feeds into product development, and (4) potential future monetization as a B2B network for non-Ather vehicles. As Ather approaches EBITDA breakeven (target FY28), the Grid investment is a growth capex that pays off through sustained premium positioning rather than direct revenue.
