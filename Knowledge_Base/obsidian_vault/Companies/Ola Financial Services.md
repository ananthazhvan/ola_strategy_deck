# Ola Financial Services / Captive Financing

Ola Financial Services (OFS) operates as the captive financing arm of Ola Electric, providing vehicle loans and financing solutions to E2W customers. In India's 2-wheeler market, financing penetration varies significantly by price point and customer segment — for an EV scooter priced between Rs 70,000 and Rs 2,00,000, access to consumer credit is often a determining factor in purchase decisions. OFS fills the gap that traditional banks and NBFCs may be unwilling to serve, particularly for younger customers or those with thinner credit histories who form Ola Electric's core target demographic.

The captive financing model offers Ola Electric strategic advantages beyond mere sales facilitation. First, it captures financing income that would otherwise flow to external lenders, improving per-unit revenue. Second, it allows Ola to control the end-to-end customer experience from purchase to repayment, potentially integrating financing offers into the MoveOS app. Third, during demand troughs, OFS can provide promotional financing (lower down payments, extended tenures) to stimulate sales that third-party lenders might not support. Ola's Revenue Per Unit rising from Rs 151,818 (FY24) to Rs 223,902 (FY26) despite declining vehicle ASPs suggests growing non-vehicle revenue streams, of which financing income could be a component.

However, the captive financing model carries significant risks, especially in the current environment. With Ola's service crisis generating 10,000+ CCPA complaints, 500+ consumer court cases, and negative NPS, customers financing vehicles through OFS face a precarious position: they continue paying EMIs on vehicles that may be awaiting service for 7-15 days. This creates a moral hazard where OFS's credit recovery efforts contradict the service experience that Ola Electric is trying to improve. In extreme cases, vehicle repossession by a related entity amid unresolved service complaints generates legal and reputational complications that cross-contaminate both entities.

The related party transaction exposure between Ola Electric and OFS is not fully transparent in public filings. Ola Electric's IPO documents and financial statements do not separately disclose the volume of vehicles financed through OFS, the outstanding loan book, or the default rates. This lack of disclosure is notable given the 54.39% retail ownership base (Mar-2026) and the stock's 76% decline from its Rs 150 peak — minority shareholders have limited visibility into off-balance-sheet financing exposure that could crystallize if vehicle quality issues trigger loan defaults or customer litigation.
