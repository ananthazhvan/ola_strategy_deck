## Trends

### FII: 5.41% -> 3.99% (Declining)
Foreign institutional investors have been net sellers, reducing exposure from 5.41% to 3.99%. This is consistent with the market share collapse (55.81% -> 15.95%) destroying the growth thesis that justified the IPO valuation. FIIs typically lead in exiting troubled positions before retail follows.

### Retail: 52% -> 54% (Rising)
Retail holding has increased from 52% to 54%, suggesting domestic retail investors have been buying the dip. This is a classic pattern in Indian markets: retail investors provide liquidity for institutional exits. At Rs 36.1, the stock trades at a 52% discount to IPO price (Rs 76) — the "value trap" narrative.

### SoftBank: 21.98% Overhang
SoftBank holds 21.98% at a cost of Rs 50.94/share — representing a 29% unrealised loss at the current Rs 36.1 price. A position of this size (21.98% of 441.08 Cr shares = ~97 Cr shares) cannot be sold quickly without crashing the stock. Any price recovery toward SoftBank's cost basis (~Rs 51) would likely trigger SoftBank exit pressure, creating a natural ceiling.

## Key Implications

1. **SoftBank overhang**: 21.98% stake at Rs 50.94 cost basis creates a natural resistance level. Any rally toward Rs 50-60 faces SoftBank exit pressure. Ola's stock may trade in a Rs 30-50 range until SoftBank exits or the company proves sustainable profitability.
2. **Retail concentration**: 54% retail holding means the stock is retail-driven with limited institutional support. Retail tends to panic-sell during bad news, creating asymmetric downside risk.
3. **FII flight**: Continued FII selling (5.41% -> 3.99%) signals that sophisticated investors lack conviction in the turnaround story.
4. **Promoter not pledged**: Bhavish Aggarwal's 36.94% is not pledged — a positive signal of promoter commitment. However, it also means he bears the largest absolute wealth destruction.
5. **Insider vs public cost basis**: Early investors (Matrix at Rs 8.22, Tiger Global at Rs 11.70) have massive gains. Late-stage investors (SoftBank at Rs 50.94, Alpha Wave at Rs 62.38) are underwater. This split creates divergent exit incentives among large holders.
