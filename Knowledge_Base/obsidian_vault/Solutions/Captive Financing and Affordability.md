## The Affordability Problem

India's E2W market is 86% mass-market (≤Rs 100,000). Ola's S1 X at Rs 69,999-89,999 competes directly with ICE scooters (Honda Activa Rs 75,000, TVS Jupiter Rs 72,000, Hero Splendor Rs 68,000). At price parity, the TCO advantage (45% lower running cost) should make EVs the obvious choice. But:

1. **Upfront price is the barrier** — Even at parity, the Indian consumer sees Rs 69,999 as a major purchase decision. ICE scooters have 40+ years of trust; EVs have 5.
2. **Financing access is worse for EV buyers** — Banks apply higher interest rates to EVs (perceived technology risk, uncertain resale value). Typical EV loan: 14-18% vs ICE: 10-14%.
3. **Total cost advantage takes 2-3 years to realize** — At Rs 1/km running cost savings, a daily 30km commuter saves Rs 900/month. But the upfront premium (if any) and higher interest rates eat into this.

## The 3-Product Captive Financing Suite

### Product 1: Ola Battery Lease (Target: 30% attach rate)
**How it works:**
- Customer buys the scooter WITHOUT the battery: Rs 49,999 (S1 X 2 kWh body only)
- Battery leased separately: Rs 999/month for 2 kWh pack, Rs 1,299/month for 3 kWh
- Lease includes: battery replacement if capacity drops below 80%, free charging at Ola Hypercharger network, insurance
- After 5 years: option to buy battery at 20% residual value (Rs 12,000), or upgrade to new chemistry

**Why it works for Ola:**
- Upfront price drops from Rs 69,999 to Rs 49,999 — instantly the cheapest EV in India
- Battery lease creates Rs 12,000-15,600/year recurring revenue per customer
- Battery return at end-of-lease gives Ola cells for second-life stationary storage (grid BESS)
- Customer is locked into Ola ecosystem for battery servicing
- LFP chemistry's 3,000+ cycle life makes this economically viable (NMC couldn't support 5-year lease)

**Why it works for customers:**
- Upfront price 29% lower than outright purchase
- "Battery anxiety" eliminated — if battery degrades, Ola replaces it (lease terms guarantee ≥80% capacity)
- Upgrade path: new chemistry after 5 years without buying a new scooter

### Product 2: Ola Pay-Per-Km (Target: 15% attach rate)
**How it works:**
- Zero upfront cost for scooter + battery
- Customer pays Rs 1.49/km (all-inclusive: vehicle, battery, maintenance, insurance, charging)
- Minimum monthly commitment: Rs 1,500 (1,000 km)
- After 60,000 km (5 years): ownership transfers to customer for Rs 1

**Why it works:**
- Fleet operators (Zomato, Swiggy, Amazon): predictable per-delivery cost, zero capex
- Daily commuters: if they ride <30km/day, they only pay for what they use
- Ola's margin: Rs 1.49/km × 60,000 km = Rs 89,400 lifetime revenue per customer vs Rs 69,999 outright sale (28% higher)
- Full-service bundle means Ola controls the entire customer experience — service, charging, insurance

### Product 3: Ola Care Subscription (Target: 25% attach rate)
**How it works:**
- Rs 499/month: all-inclusive maintenance + insurance + roadside assistance + annual detail
- Rs 799/month: above + free charging at Ola Hypercharger network + priority service booking
- Cancel anytime. 30-day notice.

**Why it works:**
- Recurring revenue: Rs 6,000-9,600/year per subscriber
- At 50,000 subscribers: Rs 30-48 Cr annual recurring revenue
- Service cost predictability: subscription model funds the Hub & Spoke network
- Customer retention: subscribers are 3x more likely to buy their next vehicle from Ola

## The Financing Infrastructure

### Capital Structure
- **OFS equity infusion:** Rs 100 Cr from Ola Electric treasury
- **Bank partnership:** Rs 100 Cr credit line from SBI/ICICI/HDFC at 9-10% (secured against vehicles)
- **Asset-backed securitization:** Bundle lease receivables and sell to debt markets after 12-month track record
- **Target lending rate to customers:** 12-14% (2-4% spread over cost of funds)

### Technology Platform
- **Ola Finance app:** Loan application, eKYC, credit scoring, eSign, disbursement — fully digital, <24 hours
- **Alternative credit scoring:** Use Ola app data (ride patterns, charging behavior, service history) to underwrite customers traditional banks reject
- **Auto-debit integration:** UPI auto-pay for monthly lease/subscription payments
- **Default management:** Remote immobilization capability (MoveOS-integrated) for severe delinquency (>90 days)
