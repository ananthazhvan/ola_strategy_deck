> [!insight] The Single Most Important Thing
> The PM E-DRIVE subsidy of Rs 5,000/E2W expires Jul 31, 2026 — 2.5 months from now. This Rs 5,000 represents 123% of Ola's current per-unit EBITDA profit (Rs 4,050 at 4.5% margin on Rs 90,000 ASP). Absorbing the full subsidy loss would push Ola back to negative EBITDA. Passing it through to customers (Rs 5,000 price increase on a Rs 69,999 S1 X) would destroy demand. The solution is a 3-part architecture: (1) Cost-side hardening to reduce the per-unit impact from Rs 5,000 to Rs 2,000, (2) State-level subsidy arbitrage to recover Rs 1,500/unit, (3) Product-mix optimization to absorb the remaining Rs 500 through margin-rich accessories and Ola Care subscriptions.

State subsidies: TN Rs 10,000-20,000, Delhi up to Rs 30,000, KA exemption ended, GJ subsidy discontinued.

# Post-Subsidy Pricing Architecture

**Deadline:** Jul 31, 2026
**Principle:** Do not pass the subsidy loss to the customer. Absorb it through cost structure, state arbitrage, and mix optimization.

---

## The Math: Why This Is Existential

Ola's EBITDA turned positive for the first time in Q3 FY26 (+2.1%) after 5 years of losses. The achievement was fragile: it required OpEx slashing of 42% (Rs 840 Cr → Rs 484 Cr), Gigafactory cell cost reduction ($130 → $85/kWh), and S1 X's simpler-build economics. The PM E-DRIVE Rs 5,000/unit removal hits this fragile structure directly:

| Scenario | Per-Unit EBITDA Impact | Annual Impact (at 180K units) |
|----------|----------------------|------------------------------|
| Full pass-through to customer | -Rs 5,000 ASP hit → demand destruction | Volume decline → fixed cost per unit rises → death spiral |
| Full absorption by Ola | EBITDA falls from +4.5% to -1.1% | Rs 90 Cr annual EBITDA swing from positive to negative |
| **3-part architecture (target)** | **Net impact: -Rs 500/unit** | **EBITDA stays positive at +4.0%** |

---

## Part 1: Cost-Side Hardening (Reduce Impact by Rs 3,000/unit)

### 1A. Gigafactory Cell Cost Reduction (Rs 1,200/unit saved)
- Current: $80/kWh LFP 46100. Target: $72/kWh by Dec 2026.
- Path: 6 GWh → 8 GWh utilization improves fixed cost absorption. Electrode manufacturing yield improvement from 85% to 92%.
- Battery is ~35% of BOM (~Rs 31,500 at Rs 90,000 ASP). 10% cell cost reduction = Rs 3,150/vehicle. Target achieving Rs 1,200.

### 1B. Gen 3 Platform BOM Optimization (Rs 800/unit saved)
- Gen 3 targets 80%+ component commonality (up from 68.85% on Gen 2).
- Supplier consolidation: reduce 400+ suppliers to 250. Volume discounts on common parts.
- LFP pack redesign: fewer cells per pack (higher energy density 46100 format).

### 1C. Logistics & Distribution Efficiency (Rs 500/unit saved)
- Hub & Spoke service reduces parts logistics cost. Central warehouse in Krishnagiri.
- Factory-gate pickup for large dealers. Reduce outbound freight by 30%.

### 1D. PLI Disbursement Acceleration (Rs 500/unit recovered)
- Auto PLI: 13-18% of "determined sales value." Only 9% disbursed in 3+ years. Aggressive compliance filing to unlock pending disbursements.
- Cell PLI: Milestone-based. Hit 6 GWh target to unlock next tranche.

## Part 2: State-Level Subsidy Arbitrage (Recover Rs 1,500/unit)

The central subsidy is dying, but state subsidies are diverging — creating geographic winners and losers.

### Strategy: Sales Focus Reallocation
| State | State Subsidy per E2W | Ola's Current Sales Mix | Target Sales Mix | Net Benefit/Unit |
|-------|----------------------|------------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Tamil Nadu (home) | Rs 10,000-20,000 + 100% road tax exemption | 25% | 35% | +Rs 2,500 |
| Delhi | Up to Rs 30,000 (tapering) + 100% road tax | 5% | 15% | +Rs 3,000 |
| Maharashtra | Rs 10,000 + 100% road tax + toll waivers | 15% | 20% | +Rs 1,500 |
| Karnataka | Road tax exemption ENDED Apr 2026 | 20% | 10% | -Rs 2,000 |
| Gujarat | Subsidy discontinued 2025 | 10% | 5% | -Rs 1,500 |
| Rest of India | Variable | 25% | 15% | Neutral |

**Net effect:** Shifting 20% of sales mix from negative/poor-subsidy states to high-subsidy states recovers ~Rs 1,500/unit blended average.

**Execution:**
- Redirect marketing spend to TN, Delhi, Maharashtra
- Offer "State Incentive Bonus" — pass through Rs 2,000 of the state subsidy as an additional customer discount in target states
- Aggressive dealer recruitment in high-subsidy states
- TN factory-gate delivery with registration in TN for maximum benefit

## Part 3: Product-Mix Optimization (Absorb Remaining Rs 500/unit)

### 3A. Ola Care Subscription (Rs 300/unit revenue)
- Rs 499/month all-inclusive maintenance + insurance
- Target 25% attach rate on new sales
- Per-unit lifetime value: Rs 12,000 over 2 years. Present value per unit sold: ~Rs 2,400.
- Recognized as recurring revenue, not one-time — improves valuation multiple.

### 3B. Accessories & Personalization (Rs 200/unit margin)
- Gen 3 accessories: premium seat, top box, phone mount, body graphics
- Sold at experience centre during purchase. 40% margin on accessories.
- Target Rs 500 accessories revenue per unit = Rs 200 incremental margin.

### 3C. Charging Installation Service (optional)
- Home charging point installation: Rs 3,999. Partner with local electricians.
- Margin: Rs 800/installation. 15% attach rate = Rs 120/unit.

## Contingency: If Cost Reductions Don't Materialize Fast Enough

**Plan B (Months 1-3 if EBITDA goes negative):**
1. Further OpEx cut: Rs 484 Cr → Rs 420 Cr (additional 13% reduction from marketing freezes, HQ consolidation)
2. S1 X price increase to Rs 74,999 (+Rs 5,000). Accept volume decline to 8,000-10,000 units/month.
3. Accelerate B2B battery sales — sell cells at near-zero margin to generate cash flow independent of scooter demand

**Plan C (Months 3-6 if cash runway drops below 12 months):**
1. Strategic minority investment from a battery/cell partner (CATL, BYD, or Reliance New Energy)
2. Sell 10-15% stake in Gigafactory subsidiary at Rs 3,000-4,000 Cr valuation
3. Monetize TN state incentives: SGST reimbursement securitization
