## The Diagnosis: Why Ola Failed

Ola Electric's collapse from 55.81% market share (Apr-22) to 8.6% (Oct-25) was not caused by one thing — it was a cascade where each failure amplified the next:

1. **D2C model without service infrastructure** — Only 300 of 935 experience centres had repair capability (32%). When quarterly volume crossed 100K units, the 7-15 day repair backlog created a trust collapse no marketing could reverse.
2. **Product quality sacrificed for speed** — 189 consumer cases at IPO became 500+ within 18 months. Courts cited "pattern of neglect." Warranty provisions grew 5.3x.
3. **Subsidy dependency exposed** — When FAME-II expired (Mar-2024), the 87.5% subsidy cut forced ASP from Rs 1,35,000 to Rs 98,000, and Ola had no cost structure to absorb it.
4. **Legacy OEMs had structural advantages Ola couldn't match** — TVS (1,000+ dealers, 95% parts), Bajaj (1,480+ dealers, Rs 400/visit, Rs 38,552 Cr reserves), Hero (6,000+ dealers, 5-year warranty).

The root cause was strategic: Ola tried to out-compete 40-year incumbents at their own game (volume, distribution, service) with a startup's cost structure and no trust inheritance.

### Pillar 3: Rebrand Through Truth (Months 3-18)
The old "tech-bro futurism" marketing is dead. The new brand is built on radical transparency.

→ **[[Solutions/Marketing Campaign Radical Transparency]]** — Gigafactory live-stream, service dashboard, engineer-led content

### Pillar 4: Grow Differently (Months 6-24)

→ **[[Solutions/GTM Strategy Project Horizon]]** — 4-phase GTM: Fix → Scooter Relaunch → Motorcycles → Export

### Pillar 5: Bring Back the Money (Months 1-18)
