# Investor Confidence Restoration Strategy

**Timeline:** 18 months to full capital markets rehabilitation
**Goal:** Stock price recovery to Rs 67+ enabling non-dilutive capital raise

---

## The Diagnosis: Why Investors Abandoned Ola

### Reason 1: Trust Destruction Was Total
Investors don't just look at financials — they look at management credibility. When Ola's management:
- Denied service crisis severity on earnings calls while CCPA was issuing show-cause notices
- Claimed "Hyperservice 80% same-day resolution" with no third-party verification
- Presided over 500+ consumer court cases where judges found "pattern of neglect"

...institutional investors concluded management was either disconnected from reality or unwilling to be honest. Both are fatal.

### Reason 2: No Credible Path to Profitability
At IPO, Ola projected a path to profitability through volume — sell 1M+ scooters/year, spread fixed costs, achieve operating leverage. The market share collapse (55.81% → 8.6%) destroyed this thesis. The new thesis (margin paradox, breakeven at 15K units) is real but hasn't been communicated or believed.

### Reason 3: Pre-IPO Investor Overhang
SoftBank (21.98%, 29% underwater) is a forced seller at any price recovery. Tiger Global (6.03%, 208% gain) has every incentive to exit. The market knows ~30% of shares want to be sold on any uptick — this caps the stock.

### Reason 4: Ather Is Valued Higher
Ather Energy: Rs 3,672 Cr revenue, still loss-making (-Rs 517 Cr), valued at $2.63B. Ola Electric: same revenue, owns a Gigafactory, EBITDA positive, valued at $1.56B. The market is saying: "Ather's brand is worth more than Ola's factory." This is a communication failure, not a business reality failure.

## Phase 1: Immediate Credibility Signals (Months 1-3)

### Action 1: Replace the Earnings Call Format
**Current:** Bhavish Aggarwal solo presentation, scripted remarks, no analyst Q&A on difficult topics.
**New format:**
- CEO + CFO + Head of Service + Head of Quality all present
- 70% of time allocated to unscripted Q&A
- Every metric reported with verifiable source: VAHAN registration data (market share), Google Play Store (customer sentiment), 3-month trailing NPS
- "Things that went wrong this quarter" — a mandatory section of every earnings presentation where management admits failures and explains fixes
- Publish the earnings transcript raw — no editing, no "forward-looking statements" legal boilerplate hiding

### Action 2: Third-Party Verification of All Metrics
- Quarterly service audit by independent firm (KPMG/E&Y/Deloitte). Results published in full.
- Monthly VAHAN registration data reconciliation against Ola's reported numbers
- Quarterly quality report with failure rates by component, verified by ARAI
- "Trust but verify" becomes the investor relations philosophy

### Action 3: Management Skin in the Game
- Bhavish Aggarwal publicly commits to zero share sales for 24 months
- Executive compensation tied to 3 metrics: (1) market share recovery, (2) EBITDA margin, (3) customer NPS
- Board addition: one independent director with automotive manufacturing experience (ex-Toyota, ex-Maruti)

### The "Ather Valuation Gap" Closure
At Rs 67 stock price, Ola's market cap = Rs 29,550 Cr (~$3.6B). This would close the valuation gap with Ather ($2.63B) and reflect the Gigafactory asset value that Ather doesn't have. The battery-first pivot narrative is the mechanism — Ola must be re-rated as an energy infrastructure company, not a scooter OEM.

## Phase 3: Strategic Capital Entry (Months 12-18)

### Step 1: SoftBank Block Trade (Managed Exit)
- SoftBank's 21.98% stake (810.4M shares) at Rs 50.94 cost basis = Rs 4,128 Cr invested
- At Rs 67 stock price: value = Rs 5,430 Cr. SoftBank at 32% gain — acceptable exit.
- Structure: Block trade to 3-4 strategic investors (not public market). Candidates:
  - **Reliance New Energy** — Wants cell manufacturing + storage capability. Rs 75,000 Cr committed to new energy.
  - **CATL / BYD** — Strategic interest in Indian cell manufacturing. Market access.
  - **ADQ / Mubadala** — Sovereign wealth funds with energy transition mandates. Long-term holders.
- Outcome: SoftBank exits cleanly. Strategic investors bring industry credibility + capital.

### Step 2: Tiger Global Orderly Exit
- Tiger Global at Rs 11.70 cost basis, 208% gain. They will sell — the question is how.
- Structure: 10% of daily volume, 6-month drip-feed. Market absorbs without disruption.
- At Rs 67: Tiger's 222.4M shares = Rs 1,490 Cr. 6-month exit = Rs 8.3 Cr/day — manageable.

### Step 3: Qualified Institutional Placement (QIP)
- After stock stabilizes at Rs 67+ with new strategic investors: Rs 2,000 Cr QIP
- Use: Gigafactory Phase 2 (12 GWh), export market entry, captive financing capitalization
- Dilution at Rs 67: 2.98 Cr shares (0.67% of outstanding). Minimal.
- Vs. raising Rs 2,000 Cr at current Rs 36: 5.56 Cr shares (1.26% dilution) — but the optics of raising at Rs 36 are terrible.
