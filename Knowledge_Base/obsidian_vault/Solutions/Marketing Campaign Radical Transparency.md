# Marketing Campaign: Radical Transparency

**Campaign duration:** 18 months (Phase 1-2 of Project Horizon)
**Budget:** Rs 80 Cr (reallocated from performance marketing)
**Core principle:** When trust is zero, transparency is the only marketing that works.

---

## The Problem With Ola's Current Marketing

Ola's historical marketing was built on four pillars that are now toxic:
1. **Futurism** — "The future of mobility" from a company with 7-15 day repair backlogs
2. **Bhavish Aggarwal as brand avatar** — Founder-centric marketing when the founder's public statements (service denials, Twitter fights) became liabilities
3. **Spec-sheet battles** — Competing on range, speed, tech features while customers cared about "will it work tomorrow"
4. **Performance ad blitz** — High-burn digital acquisition when conversion was collapsing due to word-of-mouth

Every rupee spent on the old marketing approach is a rupee that makes the brand LESS credible. The campaign must be entirely new.

---

## Campaign Architecture: 4 Pillars

### Pillar 1: THE LIVE FACTORY (Months 1-3)
**Concept:** Install 50 cameras across the Gigafactory and Futurefactory. Live-stream production 24/7 on YouTube and olaelectric.com/factory. No edits. No filters. Real manufacturing, real workers, real problems.

**Why it works:**
- No Indian manufacturer has ever done this — instant differentiation
- Forces internal accountability: when the world is watching, quality standards rise
- Content engine: 24/7 footage generates infinite social media clips
- Humanizes the company: shows 6,577 employees (3,733 on-roll + 2,844 off-roll) doing real work

**Tactical execution:**
- Launch on YouTube with a "Factory Cam" channel
- Weekly "Factory Floor Friday" — 10-minute engineer-led walkthrough of a specific production line
- Real-time production counter on website: "X cells produced today, X scooters assembled today"
- Behind-the-scenes: LFP 46100 cell production, thermal testing, Gen 3 assembly line

**Budget:** Rs 15 Cr (camera installation, streaming infrastructure, production team)

### Pillar 2: THE SERVICE DASHBOARD (Months 1-6)
**Concept:** A public, real-time dashboard at olaelectric.com/service showing: total service tickets open, average resolution time, parts availability %, customer satisfaction score, and every service center's individual metrics.

**Why it works:**
- Turns Ola's #1 weakness into a visible commitment
- Creates accountability: center managers know their numbers are public
- Gives customers a reason to check back: "Are they actually improving?"
- Preempts CCPA scrutiny: proactive transparency is the best regulatory defense

**Tactical execution:**
- Launch with current (bad) numbers — the honesty creates the impact
- Weekly "Service Report" video: Head of Service reads the numbers, explains what went wrong, what's being fixed
- Customer complaint resolution tracker: "Your complaint #14,287 — status update"
- Monthly third-party audit published in full

**Budget:** Rs 10 Cr (dashboard development, data integration, audit costs)

### Pillar 3: ENGINEERS, NOT INFLUENCERS (Months 3-18)
**Concept:** Replace celebrity endorsements and influencer campaigns with Ola's actual engineers as the face of the brand. The people who build the cells, design the motors, test the batteries become the content.

**Why it works:**
- Engineers have credibility influencers don't — especially for a brand recovering from quality failures
- "Our battery engineer explains why LFP doesn't catch fire" is more powerful than any ad
- Creates internal pride: engineers become brand heroes
- Costs 1/10th of influencer marketing

**Tactical execution:**
- "Meet Your Maker" video series: 5-minute profiles of Ola engineers
- "Ask an Engineer" weekly YouTube Live Q&A — unscripted, real questions
- Technical deep-dives: "Why 3,000 cycle life matters for your wallet" — educational content that builds trust
- Engineer social media accounts: real employees posting real work (with guidelines, not scripts)

**Budget:** Rs 25 Cr (content production, engineer training, platform management)

### Pillar 4: THE RECALL PROMISE (Months 1-12)
**Concept:** Ola publicly commits to the most aggressive recall policy in Indian automotive history: any safety-critical defect = recall within 48 hours + loaner scooter + Rs 5,000 compensation. No legal wrangling. No customer fights. Immediate action.

**Why it works:**
- Turns the #1 fear ("what if my Ola breaks?") into a reason to buy ("if it breaks, Ola fixes it immediately")
- Preempts the consumer court crisis (500+ cases, "pattern of neglect" judicial findings)
- Forces internal quality: when recalls are this expensive, QA becomes existential
- Bajaj and TVS cannot match this without disrupting their ICE service economics

**Tactical execution:**
- Public "Recall Register" — every recall listed with cause, fix, and compensation paid
- Recall response team: 24/7 hotline, guaranteed 48-hour resolution
- "We broke it. We fixed it. Here's the proof." — case study content for every major recall
- Annual Safety Report published by independent auditor

**Budget:** Rs 30 Cr (recall reserve fund, loaner fleet, compensation pool, response team)

## Channel Strategy & Budget Allocation

| Channel | Allocation | Rationale |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| YouTube (long-form) | 35% (Rs 28 Cr) | Primary content hub: live streams, engineer series, AMAs |
| Owned platforms (website, app, email) | 25% (Rs 20 Cr) | Service dashboard, recall register, production counter |
| Earned media (PR, news) | 20% (Rs 16 Cr) | "First Indian factory live-stream" is a PR story |
| Reddit / Twitter / YouTube Community | 10% (Rs 8 Cr) | Engineer AMAs, direct customer engagement |
| Instagram / Shorts (clips from long-form) | 10% (Rs 8 Cr) | Derivative content from factory streams and engineer series |
| Traditional / OOH | 0% | Not appropriate for this campaign |
| Performance / paid acquisition | 0% | Paused until trust metrics improve |
