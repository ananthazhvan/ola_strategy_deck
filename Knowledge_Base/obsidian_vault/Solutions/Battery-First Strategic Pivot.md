# Battery-First Strategic Pivot: From Scooter Company to Energy Company

**Timeline:** 30 months to full pivot execution
**Core thesis:** The Gigafactory is the crown jewel, not the scooter factory. The scooter business becomes a captive customer and technology demonstrator for the cell business — not the other way around.

---

## Why the Pivot Is Inevitable

### The Scooter Business Math Doesn't Close
At 15.95% market share and 164,000 units/year, Ola's scooter business generates ~Rs 3,672 Cr revenue. Even with 36% gross margins (best case, Gigafactory-enabled), the OpEx load (Rs 484 Cr/quarter = Rs 1,936 Cr/year) means EBITDA is barely positive (+4.5%) at current volumes. To reach 10%+ EBITDA margins, Ola needs ~30,000 units/month — a market share recovery to ~24% that requires perfect execution against TVS (23%), Bajaj (21%), Ather (16%), and Hero (11%). The probability of simultaneously beating all four is low.

### The Battery Business Math Is Compelling
The Gigafactory at 6 GWh utilization can produce cells for ~600,000 scooters/year. At $80/kWh and 6 GWh, that's $480M (Rs 4,000 Cr) in annual cell revenue. At 12 GWh (Phase 2 target, Jul-2027): Rs 8,000 Cr. At 20 GWh (full build-out): Rs 12,000+ Cr. The scooter business at peak (FY24) generated Rs 5,010 Cr. The battery business at 12 GWh would be larger than Ola's best-ever scooter revenue.

### The Policy Tailwind Is Battery-Specific
- **PM E-DRIVE 50% DVA requirement:** Forces all Indian E2W OEMs to use domestically manufactured cells. Ola is the only integrated cell manufacturer. TVS, Bajaj, Hero, Ather are all import-dependent.
- **Cell PLI 20 GWh allocation:** Ola has the largest single allocation. Rs 18,100 Cr scheme. Milestone-linked disbursements.
- **Import substitution economics:** Chinese LFP cells at $80/kWh + 15-20% import duty = $92-96/kWh landed cost for competitors. Ola's $80/kWh in-house = structural 15-20% cost advantage.

## The 3-Channel Battery Business

### Channel 1: Captive Scooter Consumption (40% of capacity at 6 GWh)
Ola's own scooter production consumes ~2.4 GWh at current volumes (164,000 units × ~15 kWh/vehicle). This is the base load — guaranteed demand that keeps the Gigafactory running. As scooter volumes recover to 300,000+ units, captive consumption rises to 4.5 GWh.

### Channel 2: B2B OEM Cell Supply (40% of capacity)
**Target customers:** Ather Energy (263,000 units), Hero Vida (150,000), and smaller E2W OEMs. These companies are import-dependent for cells. Ola offers:
- LFP 46100 cells at Rs 6,600/kWh ($80/kWh) — 15% below their current Chinese import cost
- 50% DVA compliance automatically satisfied (cells are made in Tamil Nadu)
- Supply contract: 3-year minimum, quarterly pricing reviews
- Co-branding option: "Powered by Ola Cells" on partner vehicles

**Ather as first target:** Ather is the most logical first customer. They're import-dependent, premium-positioned (so Ola cells aren't seen as "competitor parts"), and fundraising Rs 2,497 Cr in FY26 gives them capital to sign supply contracts. Ola selling cells to Ather also neutralizes the competitive tension — if Ather buys Ola cells, Ola benefits from Ather's 75% YoY growth regardless of who "wins" the scooter war.

**Addressable B2B market:** Indian E2W market at 150,000 units/month × 15 kWh/vehicle = 2.25 GWh/month = 27 GWh/year. Ola targeting 20% B2B share = 5.4 GWh.

### Channel 3: Ola Shakti — Stationary Storage (20% of capacity)
- **Residential:** Home battery storage (5-20 kWh) for solar integration and backup power. India's residential storage market is nascent but growing with rooftop solar adoption.
- **Commercial BESS:** Battery Energy Storage Systems for commercial/industrial load shifting. 50 kWh - 500 kWh installations.
- **Grid-scale:** Utility-scale storage tenders from SECI, NTPC, state discoms. India targeting 50+ GWh of grid storage by 2030.
- **Revenue potential:** Stationary storage commands 15-25% price premium over automotive cells due to longer warranty requirements. At Rs 7,500/kWh and 1.2 GWh (20% of 6 GWh), annual revenue = Rs 900 Cr.
