# GTM Strategy: Project Horizon

**Timeline:** 30 months (4 phases)
**Core insight:** Ola's first-mover advantage in scooters was squandered by service failure. The motorcycle and export markets are the second chance — and they're wide open.

---

## Phase 1: HALT & HARDEN (Months 1-6: May-Oct 2026)

**Objective:** Survive the subsidy cliff. Stop the trust hemorrhage. Build the operational foundation.

### Actions
1. **Absorb the PM E-DRIVE cliff** — Deploy the Post-Subsidy Pricing Architecture (state arbitrage, cost hardening, selective price increase on premium models, hold S1 X at Rs 69,999)
2. **Deploy Hub & Spoke service network** — 300 Ola hubs + 2,000 certified local garages. Target: 3-day turnaround, 85% parts availability within 6 months
3. **Launch Radical Transparency campaign** — Engineer-led content, live service dashboard, Gigafactory live-stream
4. **Freeze new experience centre expansion** — Redirect all retail capex to service infrastructure
5. **Complete Gen 3 platform reliability validation** — Zero-tolerance QA before any new launch

### Success Metrics
- EBITDA stays positive post-cliff (≥+2%)
- Market share ≥12% (holds above 10% trough)
- Google Play rating improves from 3.2 → 3.6
- Service turnaround: 3 days (from 7-15)
- Consumer complaints decline 30% QoQ

### Competitive Positioning
In Phase 1, Ola cedes the volume war to TVS and Bajaj. The goal is not growth — it's survival with dignity. The market will see Ola "shrinking to health" (as it already has: OpEx -42%, EBITDA positive). This creates the credible base for Phase 2.

---

## Phase 2: THE RELIABLE RELAUNCH (Months 7-12: Nov 2026-Apr 2027)

**Objective:** Re-enter the scooter market with a Gen 3 platform positioned entirely on reliability. Win back early adopters who defected.

### Actions
1. **Launch "Ola S1 Reliable" campaign** — Gen 3 S1 positioned as "The scooter that survived our 100,000 km torture test." No tech jargon. No futurism. Pure reliability proof.
2. **3-year/60,000 km warranty as standard** — Match Bajaj, exceed previous Ola offering. Extended 5-year battery warranty leveraging LFP cycle life (3,000+ cycles).
3. **Ola Care subscription** — Rs 499/month all-inclusive maintenance + insurance. Recurring revenue + locks in service relationship.
4. **Captive financing launch** — Battery leasing option: Rs 49,999 upfront + Rs 999/month battery subscription. Total cost parity with ICE.
5. **Re-open 200 highest-performing experience centres** — Those closed in Phase 1 that meet service-capability threshold

### Success Metrics
- Market share ≥18% (from 15.95%)
- Monthly unit sales ≥22,000 (breakeven + 47% buffer)
- NPS turns neutral or positive
- Google Play rating ≥3.8
- Ola Care subscribers: 50,000+
- Battery leasing penetration: 30% of new sales

### Competitive Response & Counter-Moves
- **If Bajaj drops Chetak price:** Do not match. Position S1 Reliable as premium-reliable vs Chetak's mass-cost. Highlight LFP safety advantage.
- **If TVS expands dealers:** Acknowledge publicly. "We welcome more EV service points — the market needs them." Avoid zero-sum framing.
- **If Ather launches sub-Rs 1L product:** This is the real threat. Pre-empt with S1 X Reliable at Rs 74,999 (Rs 5,000 above current S1 X, justified by warranty + service improvements).

---

## Phase 3: MOTORCYCLE ENTRY (Months 13-24: May 2027-Apr 2028)

**Objective:** Enter India's 10.8M unit/year motorcycle market where EV penetration is <1% and no incumbent has a meaningful EV motorcycle.

### Actions
1. **Launch Roadster** — Positioned as "the everyday electric motorcycle" — 150km range, Rs 1,49,000, LFP battery, 5-year warranty. Target: 25-35 year old urban commuters upgrading from 125cc ICE.
2. **Launch Adventure and Cruiser** — 6-month staggered releases. Diamondhead as halo product (low volume, high visibility).
3. **Motorcycle-only experience centres** — 50 flagship stores in top 10 cities. Differentiated from scooter retail.
4. **Fleet partnerships** — Zomato, Swiggy, Amazon last-mile delivery fleets. B2B volume provides baseload demand.
5. **Motorcycle-specific service training** — 500 certified motorcycle technicians. Separate from scooter service network.

### Success Metrics
- Motorcycle market share: 5% (50,000+ units/year)
- Roadster: 25,000+ units in first year
- Fleet B2B contracts: 10,000+ units committed
- Motorcycle contribution margin positive within 12 months

### Why This Works
Motorcycle buyers are brand-loyal to Royal Enfield, Hero, Honda — but those brands have NO EV motorcycles. This is a greenfield. Ola's Gen 2/3 platform commonality (68.85% components) means motorcycle development cost is 60% lower than building from scratch. The Roadster at 2% of Q4 FY26 mix is already in market — this is acceleration, not invention.

---

## Phase 4: EXPORT OFFENSIVE (Months 18-30: Nov 2027-Oct 2028)

**Objective:** Enter tropical emerging markets where LFP chemistry's thermal stability (45C+) is a structural competitive advantage against Chinese NMC imports.

### Actions
1. **Africa (Nigeria, Kenya, Rwanda):** BaaS model with swappable batteries. Partner with Ampersand/Spiro for battery swap stations. Launch Roadster commercial variant for delivery fleets.
2. **SE Asia (Indonesia, Philippines, Vietnam):** CKD assembly to bypass import duties. Price at 20% below Chinese imports. Leverage India manufacturing cost advantage.
3. **LATAM (Brazil):** Local assembly partnership. Leverage Brazil's EV import duty structure that favors local assembly.
4. **Export-specific product:** Commercial-grade electric motorcycle with swappable LFP battery. 200km range. Ruggedized for tropical conditions.

### Success Metrics
- Export revenue: Rs 500 Cr (FY28)
- Export units: 30,000+
- 3+ country operations live
- BaaS swap stations: 500+ across Africa
