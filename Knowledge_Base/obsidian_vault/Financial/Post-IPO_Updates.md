# Ola Electric Post-IPO Reality Check (Aug 2024 - May 2026)

> [!insight] The Single Most Important Thing: The post-IPO period revealed a company in structural crisis — market share collapsed from ~35% to 8.2%, stock crashed from ~Rs 150 to ~Rs 29. Yet the same period saw a remarkable operational restructuring: gross margins hit 34.3%, EBITDA turned positive (+4.5%), and breakeven dropped to 15,000 units/month. The IPO capital (Rs 5,429 Cr) funded the Gigafactory that enabled this turnaround.

## 1. The Market Share Collapse & Competitor Triumph
* **The Fall:** Ola's E2W market share plummeted from **~35% in early 2024 to just 8.2% by April 2026**. Total FY26 sales were only 164,000 units (less than half of FY25). 
* **The Winners:** [[TVS Motor]] (~24% share) and [[Bajaj Auto]] (~22% share) capitalized on Ola's service failures using their legacy dealer networks (1,000+ and 1,480+ respectively).
* **The Ather Threat:** [[Ather Energy]] overtook Ola in revenue in Q2 FY26 and actually surpassed Ola in total market capitalization ($2.63 Billion for Ather vs. $1.56 Billion for Ola by early 2026).
* **Stock Crash:** Driven by volume loss, Ola's stock (NSE:OLAELEC) crashed to ~₹29 by mid-2026, down over 52% from its post-IPO highs.

## 2. The Service Center Crisis (The Trust Deficit)
* **CCPA Intervention:** In October 2024, the [[Central Consumer Protection Authority]] (CCPA) issued a show-cause notice to Ola after receiving nearly 10,000 complaints on the National Consumer Helpline (citing repair delays and delivery failures).
* **Government Scrutiny:** The [[Ministry of Heavy Industries]] asked [[ARAI]] to investigate if Ola was violating the warranty and service conditions required to receive [[FAME]]/[[Market/PM E-DRIVE Subsidy]] subsidies. 
* **The "Hyperservice" Pivot:** By Q3 FY26, Ola claimed to have reduced its service backlog by 50% using AI-enabled diagnostics, resolving 80% of tickets on the same day. 

## 3. Financial Restructuring & Margin Paradox
* **Record Margins on Low Volume:** Despite crashing sales, Ola reported a massive **34.3% gross margin** in Q3 FY26. 
* **Cost Reset:** They slashed operating expenses (OpEx) from a peak of ₹840 Cr (Q4 FY25) to ₹484 Cr (Q3 FY26), targeting a steady state of ₹250-300 Cr.
* **New Breakeven:** This aggressive cost-cutting lowered their EBITDA breakeven to just **15,000 units per month** (down from much higher previous targets).

## 4. Gigafactory & Product Mix Updates
* **The LFP Pivot:** In early 2026, Ola announced a pivot away from the 4680 [[Technology/Cobalt Supply Risk and LFP Pivot]] Bharat Cell to a new **LFP Gen 1 cell in a larger 46100 format**. LFP is cheaper, doesn't use cobalt/nickel, and is more thermally stable for Indian climates.
* **Production Yield:** The [[Technology/Gigafactory_Updates]] doubled cell production QoQ in Q3 FY26 to 72,418 cells. The factory reached 2.5 GWh installed capacity, scaling toward 6 GWh.
* **B2B Expansion:** To offset the drop in scooter sales, Ola began exploring deals to supply these battery cells to *other* domestic and global automakers, and launched "Ola Shakti," a residential home battery storage system.

## Connections

- [[Service Crisis]] destroyed trust — 10,000+ CCPA complaints, 7-15 day repair backlogs
- [[Market/FAME_II_Scheme]] expiry removed Rs 40,000/vehicle subsidy, compressing margins and forcing ASP cuts
- [[Global Overcapacity]] (900 GWh excess) pressured cell prices to $80/kWh
- [[Legacy Pincer]] — TVS (1,000+ dealers) + Bajaj (1,480+ dealers) captured 44% combined share

- Stock crashed ~80% from Rs 150 peak to Rs 29 trough
- [[Ather Energy]] surpassed Ola in market cap ($2.63B vs $1.56B)
- EBITDA turned positive despite 64% volume decline
