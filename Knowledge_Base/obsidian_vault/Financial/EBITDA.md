# EBITDA

> [!insight] The Single Most Important Thing
> Ola's EBITDA narrative is defined by massive early losses driven by aggressive expansion and heavy marketing, followed by a sudden structural shift toward profitability triggered by the in-house production of battery cells at the Gigafactory.

> [!data] Source: CASH_FLOW_AND_DERIVED.csv
> Operating EBITDA margins were deeply negative in early years due to the reliance on imported cells and heavy promotional spending to secure market share during the [[Hypergrowth]] phase. 

> [!data] Source: FINANCIAL_METRICS.csv
> In-house cell manufacturing (reaching $80/kWh parity) structurally altered the unit economics. Battery cost as a percentage of BOM plummeted from 35% to ~22%, turning unit-level EBITDA positive.
