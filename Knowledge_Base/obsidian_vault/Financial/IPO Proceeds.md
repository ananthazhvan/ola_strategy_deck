# IPO Fresh Issue Allocation

> [!insight] The Single Most Important Thing: The Rs 5,429 Cr IPO (Aug 2024) was a lifeline, not a growth event. Proceeds were primarily used to (1) fund Gigafactory construction (Rs 1,226 Cr for Phase 2), (2) repay debt (Rs 800 Cr borrowings), (3) fund R&D (Rs 1,600 Cr). Without it, Ola would have been technically insolvent by FY25 given negative reserves of Rs 2,909 Cr.

> [!data] Source: CASH_FLOW_AND_DERIVED.csv — IPO proceeds of Rs 5,429 Cr in FY25 enabled borrowing reduction from Rs 5,684 Cr to Rs 3,556 Cr.

> [!data] Source: BALANCE_SHEET.csv — Reserves swung from -Rs 2,909 Cr (FY24) to +Rs 732 Cr (FY25) solely due to IPO infusion.

> [!data] Source: IPO Proceeds table — Gigafactory Phase 2 capex: Rs 1,226 Cr. Debt repayment: Rs 800 Cr. R&D: Rs 1,600 Cr.

> [!data] Source: BALANCE_SHEET.csv — Share count increased from 1,955 Cr to 4,411 Cr post-IPO (+125% dilution).

## Significance

1. **Gigafactory Phase 2** — Scaling from 5 GWh to 20 GWh requires significant capex
2. **Debt reduction** — ₹800 Cr to reduce interest burden (₹1,079M finance cost in FY23)
3. **R&D commitment** — ₹1,600 Cr signals continued investment in platform, cell tech, new products
4. **OFS context** — Major investors (SoftBank, Tiger) partially exiting; founder not selling fresh shares

## Connections

### Causes
- [[Technology/Gigafactory_Updates]] Phase 2 capex requirement of Rs 1,226 Cr drove the need for fresh equity
- Cumulative losses of Rs 6,116 Cr (FY22-FY25) made debt financing impossible
- [[PLI ACC]] disbursement lag (per POLICY_REGULATORY_TIMELINE) meant Ola couldn't rely on subsidies
- Borrowings peaked at Rs 5,684 Cr (Mar-24) — debt covenants may have forced deleveraging

### Effects
- Share count grew from 1,955 Cr to 4,411 Cr — existing holders diluted 125%
- Debt reduced from Rs 5,684 Cr to Rs 3,556 Cr (deleveraging)
- Reserves reversed from -Rs 2,909 Cr to +Rs 732 Cr (solvency restored)
- R&D budget of Rs 1,600 Cr funded [[Technology/LFP_46100_Cell]] cell development and [[Opportunities/Roadster]] motorcycle
- [[Companies/SoftBank Vision Fund]] (21.98% pre-IPO) and [[Companies/Tiger Global]] (6.03%) partially exited via OFS

### Amplifiers
- OFS by major investors signaled limited confidence in near-term value creation
- 189 consumer court cases disclosed in DRHP proved to be the "tip of the iceberg" (500+ by May 2026)
- Post-IPO, the stock crashed ~80% from Rs 150 to Rs 29, making secondary dilution punitive
