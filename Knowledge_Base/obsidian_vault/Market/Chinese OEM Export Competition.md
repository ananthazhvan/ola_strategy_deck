# Chinese OEM Export Competition

Chinese electric two-wheeler manufacturers represent the most significant competitive threat to Ola Electric's export ambitions. The structural advantage Chinese OEMs hold originates from China's dominant position in global battery cell manufacturing -- 4,200 GWh of capacity in 2026, with approximately 900 GWh in excess supply. This overcapacity has driven global cell prices to $80/kWh for LFP packs, and Chinese OEMs, with direct access to CATL and BYD cells at or below these prices, start with a raw cost advantage that Ola -- even with its Gigafactory -- struggles to match.

In every major export market Ola targets, Chinese OEMs or Chinese-backed local players already hold entrenched positions. VinFast dominates Vietnam (500K unit market, 25% CAGR) with government backing and an established dealer network, making Ola's opportunity there "Low." In Africa, Chinese-backed players Spiro, MAX, and Ampersand already offer Battery-as-a-Service models for commercial delivery -- precisely the products Ola lacks. In Brazil (400K units, 20% CAGR), 35-60% import duties require local assembly, which Chinese OEMs are better positioned to execute given their experience in global manufacturing footprints.

The competitive dynamic becomes even more challenging when export-market product requirements are considered. African markets demand heavy-duty motorcycles with swappable batteries for commercial use -- a product segment where Chinese OEMs already have established offerings. Ola's four announced motorcycle models (Diamondhead, Adventure, Roadster, Cruiser) target the consumer segment, not the commercial delivery use case that dominates African demand. Until Ola develops a commercial-grade motorcycle with BaaS capability -- and achieves global cell cost parity -- Chinese OEMs will maintain an unassailable cost and product-positioning advantage in every export market.

## Key Data Points

- China's global cell capacity: 4,200 GWh (2026) with ~900 GWh excess
- Global LFP pack price: $80/kWh (2026) -- Chinese OEMs source at or below this level
- Ola in-house cell cost: $85/kWh (NMC+LFP, Q4 FY26) -- above global price
- Vietnam: VinFast dominates 500K unit market; Ola opportunity rated "Low"
- Africa: Spiro, MAX, Ampersand already offer BaaS commercial motorcycles
- Nigeria: Existing India competitors (MAX, Spiro) already present
- Brazil: 35-60% import duties require local assembly -- Chinese OEMs have global manufacturing experience
- Philippines: Only major market without significant Indian or Chinese competition -- "Medium" opportunity
- Ola's motorcycle product line targets consumer segment, not commercial delivery use case
