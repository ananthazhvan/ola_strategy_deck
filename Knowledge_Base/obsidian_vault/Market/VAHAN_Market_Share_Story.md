# VAHAN Market Share Story: 48-Month Timeline

> [!insight] The Single Most Important Thing
> Over 48 months (Apr 2022 to Mar 2026), Ola Electric went from commanding 55.81% market share to 15.95% — a 39.86 percentage point loss — while legacy players (TVS+Bajaj+Hero) surged from 20.93% to 65.23%. The crossover came in Feb 2024 when legacy (42.51%) first exceeded Ola (41.92%). Ola never led again. This is one of the fastest market share destructions in Indian automotive history.

> [!data] Source: E2W_MONTHLY_REGISTRATIONS.csv — 48 months of VAHAN registration data (Apr-22 through Mar-26). Ola peak share: 55.81% (Apr-22). Ola trough share: 8.6% (Oct-25). Ola recent: 15.95% (Mar-26). Legacy peak: 65.23% (Mar-26).

> [!data] Source: EV_SEGMENT_COMPARISON.csv — FY26 full-year shares: TVS 23.0% (leader), Bajaj 21.0%, Ather 16.0%, Ola 15.9%, Hero 11.0%. Ola is now the #4 player — from #1 in 24 months.

## The Complete Timeline

### Phase 1: Ola Dominance (Apr 2022 - Feb 2024)
Ola held market leadership from the earliest data point. The first-mover advantage from aggressive pricing, FAME-II subsidies, and strong brand buzz created a dominant position:

| Month | Ola Share | Legacy Share | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Apr-22 | 55.81% | 20.93% | Ola peak — commanding lead |
| Mar-23 | 43.19% | 37.81% | Legacy closing gap |
| Aug-23 | 39.27% | 41.62% | Legacy takes first lead? |
| Feb-24 | **41.92%** | **42.51%** | **THE CROSSOVER** — legacy overtakes Ola |
| Mar-24 | 44.69% | 40.23% | Ola briefly recovers (FAME-II expiry pull-forward) |

The Feb-2024 crossover was the inflection point — legacy never looked back.

### Phase 2: Accelerating Collapse (Mar 2024 - Oct 2025)
Post-FAME-II expiry, Ola's service crisis and subsidy erosion triggered a 20-month freefall:

| Month | Ola Share | Ola Units | Key Event |
|-------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Mar-24 | 44.69% | 53,320 | FAME-II expiry rush — last "normal" month |
| Jun-24 | 34.68% | 30,000 | Post-subsidy hangover |
| Oct-24 | 26.40% | 26,000 | **CCPA show-cause notice** |
| Nov-24 | ~22% | 25,000 | **TVS overtakes Ola for first time** |
| Dec-24 | 22.64% | 24,000 | **Bajaj also overtakes Ola** — Ola drops to #3 |
| Mar-25 | 19.24% | 23,435 | Ola share below 20% for first time |
| Jul-25 | 11.67% | 14,000 | Below 15% |
| Oct-25 | **8.60%** | 8,000 | **All-time trough** — Ola now #4 or #5 |
| Nov-25 | 8.70% | 7,567 | Registration trough (lowest absolute volume) |

Over 20 months (Mar-24 to Nov-25): Ola registrations fell 85.8% (53,320 to 7,567), market share fell 36.09pp.

### Phase 3: Partial Recovery (Nov 2025 - Mar 2026)
Ola staged a modest recovery from the trough as Gigafactory cells improved margins and the Hyperservice initiative improved turnaround:

| Month | Ola Share | Ola Units | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Nov-25 | 8.70% | 7,567 | Trough |
| Dec-25 | ~10% | ~10,000 | Modest recovery begins |
| Jan-26 | ~12% | ~15,000 | Steady improvement |
| Feb-26 | ~14% | ~20,000 | Accelerating |
| Mar-26 | **15.95%** | **23,713** | Recent — still far from peak |

Ola recovered to 15.95% share but remains stuck in the 8-16% range — a fraction of its 55.81% peak.

## Connections

### Causes
- [[Risks/Customer Experience Gap|Service crisis (7-15 day turnaround, 60-75% parts)]] drove customers to reliable alternatives
- [[Risks/Subsidy Dependency|FAME-II expiry (Mar-2024)]] removed Rs 40,000/vehicle subsidy — demand shock
- [[Risks/CCPA_Regulatory_Intervention|CCPA show-cause notice (Oct-2024)]] — official validation of complaints
- [[Companies/Competitor_Firepower|Legacy OEMs with superior service networks]] captured every lost customer

### Effects
- Ola went from category leader (55.81%) to #4 player (15.95%) — 39.86pp loss
- TVS became market leader (23.0%) by absorbing Ola's service-driven defectors
- Bajaj grew 23.3x from 1,500 to 35,000 units/month through lowest-cost service
- Brand trust destroyed — recovery requires sustained service excellence
- Revenue collapsed 71% peak-to-trough, forcing restructuring

### Amplifiers
- CCPA action was the catalytic event — broke consumer inertia post-Oct-2024
- Negative media coverage across ET/Mint/BS sustained the brand damage for 2+ years
- Google Play rating 3.2 (from 4.0) vs TVS 4.2 and Ather 4.0 deters new buyers
- Negative NPS means existing owners actively warn others — compounding headwind
