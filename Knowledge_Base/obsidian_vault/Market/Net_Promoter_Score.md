# Net Promoter Score

Net Promoter Score (NPS) is the single most important quantitative metric bridging customer experience and brand valuation in the Indian E2W market. It measures the proportion of promoters (customers who would recommend the brand) minus detractors (those who would actively discourage others), producing a score from -100 to +100. Ola Electric's estimated NPS is negative -- below zero -- meaning detractors outnumber promoters, a position that creates a structural word-of-mouth headwind for every sales recovery effort.

The NPS gap between Ola and its competitors is stark. Bajaj Auto maintains an NPS in the 40-50 range, consistent with its 40+ years of service experience and the industry's lowest service cost (Rs 400/visit). TVS Motor and Ather Energy, with app ratings of 4.2/5.0 and 4.0/5.0 respectively, almost certainly have positive NPS scores. This differential means competitors benefit from organic customer acquisition through recommendations, while Ola must overcome negative word-of-mouth with every transaction. For a company already spending 35.4% of revenue on selling and administration expenses, the NPS penalty represents a hidden cost that inflates customer acquisition costs by an estimated 20-30%.

The financial implications of negative NPS extend beyond customer acquisition. Institutional investors track NPS as a leading indicator of brand health and future revenue trajectory. Ola's declining FII holding (5.41% to 3.99% over 9 months) and 76% stock price decline from peak are partially explained by this metric -- sophisticated investors recognize that a brand with negative NPS cannot sustain volume recovery without extraordinary service improvements. Until NPS turns positive, every quarter of volume growth will require disproportionate marketing and discount spend, compressing margins even as gross margins improve from the Gigafactory integration.
