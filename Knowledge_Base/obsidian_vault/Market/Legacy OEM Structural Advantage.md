# Legacy OEM Structural Advantage

The conventional narrative of Ola Electric's decline focuses on the service crisis, but the deeper strategic reality is that legacy OEMs (TVS, Bajaj, Hero, and to a lesser extent Ather) possess structural advantages that pure-play EV companies cannot replicate. These advantages were dormant during Ola's early dominance (47% market share in early FY24) but activated powerfully once legacy OEMs committed to EV transition.

**Manufacturing efficiency advantage**: Bajaj's 20.5% standalone EBITDA margin (highest among competitors) is built on 40+ years of Total Productive Maintenance (TPM) certified manufacturing -- 82% of Bajaj's 1,480+ dealers are TPM-certified, enabling Rs 400/service visit costs. This manufacturing DNA transfers directly to EV production, as demonstrated by Bajaj's Chetak being the only E2W with "double-digit positive" EV EBITDA margins. Pure-play EV makers without this heritage must build manufacturing competence from scratch.

**Dealer network advantage**: Hero's 6,000+ dealer network, Bajaj's 3,800+ EV touchpoints, and TVS's 1,200+ dedicated EV centers represent 30-40 years of distribution investment. These networks offer 95% parts availability (vs Ola's 60-75% at crisis peak), 1-2 day turnaround (vs Ola's 7-15), and independent hybrid mechanic access in small towns where Ola has zero presence.

**Cross-subsidy advantage**: Hero MotoCorp generates Rs 8,315 Cr/year in operating cash flow and has Rs 19,286 Cr in investments. This enables Hero to sustain Vida EV losses of Rs 900+ for every Rs 100 of revenue (-95% EBITDA margin) indefinitely. Bajaj's Rs 38,552 Cr in reserves means it can absorb EV losses for decades. Ola must fund all operations from Rs 3,516 Cr cash with a 12-18 month runway -- an asymmetric financial battle.

## Key Data Points

- Legacy combined E2W market share: from near-zero (FY23) to 65.23% (Mar 2026)
- Bajaj EBITDA margin: 20.5% (highest in industry); EV EBITDA: double-digit positive
- Bajaj reserves: Rs 38,552 Cr (24x Ola's cash); dealer network: 3,800+ EV touchpoints
- Hero dealer network: 6,000+ (largest in India); investments: Rs 19,286 Cr
- Hero Vida EBITDA margin: -95% -- but sustainable for 20+ years via parent cash flows
- Legacy parts availability: 95% vs Ola at 60-75% (during crisis)
- Legacy service turnaround: 1-2 days vs Ola at 7-15 days (during crisis)
- Bajaj service cost: Rs 400/visit vs Ola Rs 800-1,500
- TPM-certified dealers: 82% of Bajaj's 1,480+ network
