# Subsidy Cliff: July 31, 2026

> [!insight] The Single Most Important Thing
> PM E-DRIVE subsidy expires July 31, 2026 — 2.5 months from today — with zero successor scheme announced. Combined with Karnataka's road tax reversal (Apr 2026), the on-road price impact of Rs 10,000-25,000 would consume 123% of Ola's current per-unit EBITDA profit (Rs 4,050/unit at +4.5% margin). The post-subsidy era is survival of the lowest-BOM players, and Ola's vertical integration is its best defence.

> [!data] Source: POLICY_REGULATORY_TIMELINE.csv — PM E-DRIVE: Rs 10,900 Cr outlay, Rs 5,000/E2W via e-voucher, terminal date Jul-2026 (E2W). No successor scheme announced. Fund-limited — could exhaust before terminal date if demand surges.

> [!data] Source: POLICY_REGULATORY_TIMELINE.csv — FAME-II: Rs 10,000 Cr outlay, up to Rs 40,000/E2W, expired Mar-2024. EMPS-2024 bridge: Rs 500 Cr outlay, Rs 5,000/E2W, expired Sep-2024. One-way trajectory: Rs 40,000 -> Rs 5,000 -> Rs 0.

> [!data] Source: POLICY_REGULATORY_TIMELINE.csv — Karnataka ended 100% road tax exemption Apr-2026, replaced with 5-10% tiered LTT. Adds Rs 5,000-15,000 to on-road price in Bangalore and Karnataka.

> [!data] Source: E2W_MONTHLY_REGISTRATIONS.csv — Post-FAME-II analogy: total market dropped 29% single-month (119,320 to 84,463). Ola dropped 36% (53,320 to 33,963). Smaller this time (Rs 5k vs Rs 35k net reduction), but cumulative subsidy fatigue compounds.

> [!data] Source: QUARTERLY_FINANCIALS_EXT.csv — Ola EBITDA: +2.1% (Q3 FY26), +4.5% (Q4 FY26). Per-unit profit ~Rs 4,050 at current margins. Removing Rs 5,000 subsidy would wipe this out if passed through to consumers.

## The Karnataka Factor

Karnataka's reversal of 100% road tax exemption (replaced with 5-10% tiered lifetime tax, effective Apr 2026) compounds the central subsidy cliff. For a Rs 90,000-1,50,000 E2W, this adds Rs 5,000-15,000 to the on-road price. The combined price shock:

- Central subsidy removal: Rs 5,000
- Karnataka tax reinstatement: Rs 5,000-15,000
- **Combined: Rs 10,000-25,000**

This disproportionately impacts [[Companies/Ather Energy]] (Bangalore HQ, heavy Karnataka sales concentration) while [[Companies/Ola Electric]] gains a relative advantage via Tamil Nadu's continued 100% road tax exemption + Rs 10,000-20,000 subsidy through Dec 2027.

---

## State Policy Divergence (Post-Jul 2026 Landscape)

With central subsidies gone, state-level policies become the decisive competitive factor:

**Favorable States:**
- **Tamil Nadu** (Ola home state): 100% road tax exemption + Rs 10,000-20,000/E2W subsidy through Dec 2027
- **Maharashtra**: Rs 10,000/E2W + 100% tax exemption + toll waivers through 2030
- **Delhi**: Up to Rs 30,000/E2W (tapering) + 100% road tax waiver through 2030

**Unfavorable States:**
- **Karnataka**: 100% exemption replaced by 5-10% LTT (Apr 2026)
- **Gujarat**: Direct subsidy expired Mar 2026, only 1% motor vehicle tax remains

---

## Strategic Implications

1. Ola achieved EBITDA-positive at Q3 FY26 (+2.1%) and Q4 FY26 (+4.5%) — just 2-3 quarters before the cliff
2. At current margins, Rs 5,000 subsidy = 123% of per-unit profit
3. Ola must choose: absorb Rs 5,000 (near-zero margin), pass to consumers (demand risk), or achieve further cost reduction (unlikely in 2.5 months)
4. Historical parallel: post-FAME-II, volumes dropped 36% in one month and margins took 5 quarters to recover
5. **Ola's best defence**: Gigafactory integration already reduced ASP from Rs 98k to Rs 90k while turning EBITDA positive. Lowest BOM wins in a post-subsidy world.

### Causes
- [[Market/FAME_II_Scheme]] expiry (Mar-2024) set the subsidy-erosion trajectory in motion
- [[Market/PM E-DRIVE Subsidy]] terminal date Jul-2026 with no successor scheme announced
- [[Market/State_EV_Policies]] reversal of 100% road tax exemption (Apr 2026) compounds the shock
- [[ACC PLI]] disbursement lag forced Ola to front-load Rs 4,000+ Cr capex before subsidy receipts

### Effects
- Post-Jul 2026: zero central subsidy for E2W in India for the first time since 2019
- Combined price shock (Rs 10,000-25,000) could trigger H2 FY27 demand contraction (65-75% probability)
- Survival determined by BOM efficiency — Ola's vertical integration is the key differentiator
- Geographic winners/losers: Tamil Nadu gains, Karnataka loses, Ather disproportionately hurt

### Amplifiers
- Fund-limited PM E-DRIVE could exhaust before Jul 2026 if demand surges
- Cumulative fatigue from 2 years of subsidy erosion (Rs 40,000 -> Rs 5,000 -> Rs 0)
- Gujarat (Mar 2026) and Karnataka (Apr 2026) are leading indicators of state-level policy withdrawal
- ACC PLI disbursement lag means Ola is funding Gigafactory without subsidy support
