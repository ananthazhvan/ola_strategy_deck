# India EV Policy & Regulatory Landscape (2019-2030)

> [!insight] The Single Most Important Thing: India's E2W subsidy follows a one-way path to zero: Rs 40,000 (FAME-II) -> Rs 5,000 (PM E-DRIVE) -> Rs 0 after Jul 2026. This cliff is 2.5 months away. Karnataka's road tax reversal (Apr 2026) compounds the demand risk. The post-subsidy market will be a survival contest determined by BOM efficiency. Ola's Gigafactory vertical integration is the key differentiator — but the Rs 5,000 subsidy removal equals 123% of current per-unit profit.

## Central Government Schemes

### FAME-II → EMPS-2024 → PM E-DRIVE (The Subsidy Evolution)
| Scheme | Period | Outlay (₹ Cr) | E2W Subsidy | Status |
|--------|--------|---------------|-------------|--------|
| FAME-II | Apr 2019 – Mar 2024 | 10,000 | Up to ₹15,000/kWh (max ₹40,000) | **Expired** |
| EMPS-2024 (Bridge) | Apr 2024 – Sep 2024 | 500 | ₹5,000 per E2W | **Expired** |
| PM E-DRIVE | Oct 2024 – Jul 2026 (E2W) | 10,900 | ₹5,000 per E2W (e-voucher) | **Active** |

### ⚠️ CRITICAL: PM E-DRIVE Terminal Date
- **E2W subsidies end July 31, 2026** — only ~2.5 months left
- Scheme is **fund-limited**: if budget exhausts before terminal date, subsidies stop early
- E-voucher mandatory for buyers to claim subsidy
- Electric four-wheelers are **excluded** from demand incentives

### GST Structure
| Item | GST Rate | Comparison |

## State-Level Policies (as of May 2026)

### Tamil Nadu (Ola's Home State)
- **100% road tax + registration exemption** extended to Dec 2027
- E2W purchase subsidy: ₹10,000-20,000
- Interest subsidies for commercial EV loans
- **Ola advantage:** Factory in Krishnagiri benefits from state manufacturing incentives

### Maharashtra (Mumbai/Pune Market)
- **100% road tax + registration exemption** (2025-2030 policy)
- Purchase subsidy: 10-15% of ex-factory cost (₹10,000 cap for E2W)
- Toll waivers on major expressways
- Strong incentive environment

### Delhi (Capital Market)
- **100% road tax waiver** for EVs under ₹30L (until Mar 2030)
- Purchase subsidy: Up to ₹30,000 in Year 1 (tapering)
- Scrappage incentive: Up to ₹1L for replacing ICE with EV

### Karnataka (Bangalore — Key Tech Market)
- ⚠️ **Ended 100% road tax exemption** in April 2026
- New tiered Lifetime Tax: 5% (under ₹10L), 8% (₹10-25L), 10% (above ₹25L)
- Shifts focus to manufacturing investment, not demand incentives
- **Impact on Ola:** On-road price increase in Bangalore — a key market

### Gujarat
- Direct purchase subsidies **discontinued**
- Motor vehicle tax reduced to 1% (until Mar 2026) — now expired
- Registration fee waiver remains
- Weakened incentive environment

## Strategic Implications for Ola
1. **Subsidy cliff in Jul 2026:** PM E-DRIVE for E2W ends soon. Post-subsidy pricing will test true demand
2. **Karnataka policy shift:** Bangalore was a top EV market; new taxes increase on-road prices 5-10%
3. **Tamil Nadu advantage:** Home state policy remains strongest — manufacturing + demand incentives
4. **GST moat:** 5% vs 28% gives EVs structural price advantage over ICE — this is permanent
5. **PLI upside:** If Gigafactory hits milestones, PLI disbursement could add ₹500-800 Cr over scheme period
