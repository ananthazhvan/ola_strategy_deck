# Brand Perception

Ola Electric's brand perception has suffered a comprehensive collapse that goes beyond the narrower concept of trust. While [[Market/Brand Trust Deficit]] focuses specifically on the trust dimension — the willingness of customers to believe Ola will fulfill its service promises — brand perception encompasses the full landscape of how consumers, investors, media, and competitors view the Ola name.

**Quality Perception:** Ola's Net Promoter Score is estimated negative (below 0), meaning more customers actively discourage others from buying than recommend the brand. The Google Play Store rating of 3.2/5.0 with 200,000+ reviews skewing heavily toward 1-star creates a visible quality signal that every prospective buyer encounters during research. The National Consumer Helpline ranking Ola in the "Top 5 among all companies" across all sectors for complaint volume — an extraordinary negative achievement for a company with less than 1 million cumulative vehicles sold — has fundamentally redefined how Ola is perceived as a quality brand.

**Premium vs Budget Positioning:** Ola entered the market as a premium-performance EV brand (S1 Pro at Rs 1,35,000) and has been forced into a mass-market budget position (S1 X at Rs 69,999-89,999). The brand's inability to sustain premium pricing is not just a financial metric — it reflects a fundamental setback in brand hierarchy. Competitors like Ather have maintained consistent premium positioning (Rs 139,582/unit ASP) with pricing discipline, while Ola's ASP collapsed 34%. The S1 Pro+ at Rs 199,000 still exists but is crippled by its "service reputation; trust deficit" weakness — customers who can afford premium choose trusted brands over raw specifications.

**Competitor Brand Comparison:** In every dimension of brand perception, Ola trails its competitors. Ather has the highest brand perception scores with a 4.0/5.0 app rating and premium pricing discipline. TVS leads EV market share (23%) with a 4.2/5.0 app rating, leveraging its 30+ year trust inheritance. Bajaj Chetak offers "metal body; retro style; lowest service cost (Rs 400)" as its USP — a brand built on durability and value. Hero Vida's 5-year vehicle warranty signals confidence. Ola's only brand advantage — "best range and speed in class" — is neutralized by service failures.

**Investor and Media Perception:** The stock price collapse from Rs 150 peak to Rs 36.10 (76% decline) and FII holding declining to 3.99% signal institutional loss of confidence. Media coverage is predominantly negative, focused on the service crisis and CCPA action. ET, Mint, and Business Standard have covered the CCPA notice prominently, and each negative ruling or complaint escalation generates fresh negative coverage that sustains the brand damage narrative. The Ola name has become shorthand in Indian business media for a cautionary tale about scaling without infrastructure.
