## What This Means for the Strategy Deck
The "Trust Deficit" is not a qualitative feeling — it's a **quantifiable, multi-dimensional crisis** affecting:
1. **Acquisition:** New buyers choose competitors because of viral negative reviews — monthly Ola registrations fell from 53,320 (Mar-24) to 7,567 (Nov-25)
2. **Retention:** Existing owners don't recommend → word-of-mouth collapses → negative NPS accelerates the decline
3. **[[Regulatory risk]]:** [[Risks/CCPA_Regulatory_Intervention]] investigation could lead to penalties or forced recalls; [[MHI]] asked [[ARAI]] to investigate subsidy eligibility
4. **[[Valuation]]:** Stock crashed ~80% from post-IPO peak ~Rs 150 to ~Rs 29 partly due to brand perception damage

> [!data] Source: QUARTERLY_FINANCIALS_EXT.csv
> [[Revenue]] declined from peak **Rs 1,644 Cr (Q1 FY25)** to trough **Rs 450 Cr (Q2 FY26)** — a 71% decline. Yet [[gross margin]] improved from **18% to 36%** and [[EBITDA]] turned positive (**+4.5% in Q4 FY26**) for the first time. This is the [[Financial/Margin_Paradox]]: the company is restructuring into viability despite the trust crisis, but requires demand recovery to scale.

The strategy solution must address EACH complaint category, not just "improve service" generically.
