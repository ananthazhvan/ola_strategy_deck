> Ola's revenue decline of **9.9% in FY25** (Rs 5,010 Cr → Rs 4,514 Cr) masks a far deeper collapse: **quarterly revenue fell 71% from peak (Rs 1,644 Cr in Q1 FY25) to trough (Rs 470 Cr in Q4 FY26)** — then partially recovered to Rs 520 Cr. The full-year decline was cushioned by H1 FY25 being strong before the [[Risks/Service_Center_Crisis]] fully hit. But the underlying trend is brutal: **monthly sales dropped from 53,320 units (Mar-24) to 7,567 (Nov-25)** — an 85.8% decline. The decline is a direct consequence of the trust crisis, not market contraction — the total E2W market grew from 119K to 149K monthly units over the same period.

# Revenue Decline FY25

**Criticality:** 🔴 HIGH

---

## The Reversal

After explosive growth from FY22-FY24 (Rs 373 Cr → Rs 5,010 Cr), [[Companies/Ola Electric]]'s revenue **declined 9.9% in FY25** to Rs 4,514 Cr. The quarterly decline was far steeper.

> [!data] Source: PRODUCT_MIX_QUARTERLY.csv
> The revenue decline was amplified by a **product mix shift to cheaper models**. The [[Opportunities/S1 X]] (entry-level, ~Rs 70K) grew from **15% to 72% of sales**, collapsing blended [[ASP]] from **Rs 1,35,000 to Rs 90,000** (-33%). [[S1 Pro]] fell from **35% to 7%**, S1 Air from **10% to 5%**. However, the story has a positive side: [[gross margin]] doubled from **18% to 36%** because [[Technology/Gigafactory_Updates]] in-house cells cut battery cost from $130 to $85/kWh, making even cheaper scooters more profitable per unit.

---

## What Changed

### External
- [[Risks/Subsidy Dependency|FAME subsidy reduction]] (40%→15% cap) increased vehicle prices by Rs 22,784-Rs 37,106
- [[Risks/Competition|Competition intensified]] as incumbents scaled EV offerings — [[Companies/TVS Motor]] reached **23% share**, [[Companies/Bajaj Auto]] hit **21%**, [[Companies/Ather Energy]] grew **75% YoY**
- [[Market/PM E-DRIVE Subsidy]] subsidy ends **Jul 2026** — creating additional price risk for H2 FY27

### Internal
- [[Risks/Product Quality|Quality concerns]] accumulated, affecting repeat purchases and [[word-of-mouth]]
- [[Risks/Customer Experience Gap|Service gaps]] created friction in customer experience
- [[Brand Perception]] shifted from "Exciting" to "Unreliable"
- The [[Risks/CCPA_Regulatory_Intervention]] show-cause notice (Oct 2024) triggered negative media coverage across [[ET]], [[Mint]], [[Business Standard]]

> [!data] Source: E2W_MONTHLY_REGISTRATIONS.csv
> The monthly VAHAN data shows the precise mechanics of the decline. Ola registrations: **53,320 (Mar-24)** → 33,963 (Apr-24, -36% in one month post-FAME expiry) → 31,000 → 30,000 → 29,000 → 28,000 → 27,000 → 26,000 (Oct-24, CCPA month) → continuing decline to **7,567 (Nov-25)**. YoY growth went from **+146.98%** to **-69.73%**. The worst of the decline occurred while the total market grew from ~84,000 to ~149,000 — Ola lost share in a rapidly growing market.

## Implications

1. **[[Cash burn]] accelerates** — lower revenue + fixed costs = larger losses (FY25 loss: Rs 2,276 Cr, worst ever)
2. **[[Market share]] loss** — revenue declining while market growing: went from 44.69% to 15.95% share
3. **Financing dependence** — FY25 financing cash flow +Rs 5,429 Cr to stay afloat; cash at Rs 3,516 Cr (Mar-25)
4. **[[Valuation]] erosion** — stock at Rs 36.1 (80.7% below post-IPO peak ~Rs 150), market cap Rs 15,923 Cr
5. **[[Financial/Margin_Paradox]]** — despite revenue decline, the company is restructuring into viability at lower volumes

## Connections

### Causes
- **Trust crisis**: [[Risks/CCPA_Regulatory_Intervention]] notice (Oct 2024) and 10,000+ complaints destroyed buyer confidence
- **Service network failure**: 7-15 day repair backlogs vs legacy 1-2 days pushed customers to TVS/Bajaj/Ather
- **Subsidy reduction**: FAME II to EMPS (Rs 40K → Rs 5K per vehicle) forced ASP cuts of Rs 37,000
- **Competition**: TVS/Bajaj/Hero absorbed every unit Ola lost — combined legacy share at 65.23%
- **Product mix cannibalization**: S1 X at 72% of sales collapsed blended ASP from Rs 1.35L to Rs 0.90L

### Effects
- **Larger losses**: FY25 net loss Rs 2,276 Cr — 44% worse than FY24
- **Cash burn spike**: Operating cash outflow Rs 2,391 Cr in FY25 (nearly 4x FY24)
- **Stock collapse**: ~80.7% from post-IPO peak
- **Reduced investment capacity**: "Deep Negative" EBITDA prevents service network expansion or R&D
- **Recovery path emerges**: EBITDA positive (+4.5%) for first time in Q4 FY26 — the restructuring is working

### Amplifiers
- **Negative operating leverage**: 70% volume decline spread fixed costs (Rs 566 Cr depreciation, Rs 366 Cr interest) over fewer units
- **PM E-DRIVE subsidy cliff**: Jul 2026 — Rs 5,000 subsidy removal equals 123% of current per-unit profit
- **[[Market/Google Play Store Rating]] rating gap** (3.2 vs competitors 4.0-4.2) erodes D2C conversion at every research touchpoint
- **Negative NPS** turns existing owners into anti-salespeople
