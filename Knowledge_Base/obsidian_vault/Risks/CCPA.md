# Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA)

> [!insight] The Single Most Important Thing
> The CCPA show-cause notice in October 2024 was unprecedented for an EV company in India. It marked the transition of Ola's service issues from a PR/Social Media problem into a critical regulatory and financial risk.

> [!data] Source: CUSTOMER_SENTIMENT.csv
> Over 10,000 formal complaints were filed with the CCPA by October 2024. This massive volume triggered the show-cause notice, an event labeled as "Very Negative" for consumer sentiment.

> [!data] Source: SERVICE_CRISIS_TIMELINE.csv
> In October 2024, the situation hit a "CRITICAL" state. Following the CCPA notice, the Ministry of Heavy Industries (MHI) also asked ARAI to investigate subsidy eligibility violations. Ola's market share was 26% at this time, leading to a rapid decline.

> [!data] Source: risk_factors.txt
> Prior to the IPO, there were 189 cases filed in Consumer Courts (as disclosed in the DRHP in Aug 2024). Post-IPO, this number skyrocketed to an estimated 500+ cases by May 2026 across multiple district forums, often resulting in rulings mandating full refunds plus ₹10,000-₹50,000 in compensation.
