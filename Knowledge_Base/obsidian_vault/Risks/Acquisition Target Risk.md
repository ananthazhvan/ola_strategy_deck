# Acquisition Target Risk

Ola Electric's dramatic stock price collapse -- from an IPO peak of approximately Rs 150 to a current Rs 36.10, a 76% decline -- has reduced the company's market capitalisation to Rs 15,923 Cr (~$1.9 billion). At this valuation, Ola's Gigafactory assets alone (20 GWh target capacity, already at 6 GWh operational, built at significant capital investment) may be worth more to a strategic buyer than the current market capitalisation suggests, creating a classic asset-stripping or consolidation opportunity.

The shareholder structure amplifies this vulnerability. Promoter Bhavish Aggarwal holds 34.59%, meaning a bidder needs only ~15.5% more to gain effective control if the promoter does not participate. Pre-IPO investors (SoftBank Vision Fund with 21.98% pre-IPO stake at Rs 50.94/share weighted average cost, Tiger Global, and others) face lockup expiry and are likely sellers given current losses on their investment. FIIs have been consistently reducing (from 5.41% to 3.99% in 9 months), while retail investors -- who now hold 54.39% -- are typically less able to evaluate tender offers or resist a premium bid.

Potential strategic acquirers include: Mahindra & Mahindra (aspiring EV player without a dedicated 2W EV platform), Hero MotoCorp (would gain Gigafactory capacity and eliminate a competitor), or an international OEM seeking India market entry (BYD, Honda, or a European player without 2W presence in India). The discounted valuation, Gigafactory asset base, and depressed stock price create an attractive entry point that may not persist once Ola's recovery story gains credibility.
