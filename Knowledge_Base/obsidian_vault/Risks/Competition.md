## Competitive Landscape

[[Companies/Ola Electric]] faces competition from two fronts:

### Pure EV Players
- **[[Ather Energy]]** — Strong product quality, superior service, [[Companies/Hero MotoCorp]] backed
- Has been in EV space longer, built reputation for reliability
- Growing at **75% YoY** (fastest among all players), 16.0% FY26 share
- [[Companies/Ather Grid]]: 2,000+ charging points — most extensive public E2W charging network

### Incumbent ICE Manufacturers
- **[[TVS Motor]]** ([[Companies/TVS iQube|iQube]]) — 1,000+ dealer touchpoints, **95% parts availability**, 1-2 day turnaround
- **[[Bajaj Auto]]** ([[Companies/Bajaj Chetak|Chetak]]) — 1,480+ dealers, **Rs 400/visit service cost** (cheapest), already EV profitable
- **[[Companies/Hero MotoCorp]]** ([[Companies/Hero MotoCorp|Vida V1]]) — **6,000+ dealers** (20x Ola's repair-capable centers), 3,600+ fast-charging points

> [!data] Source: E2W_MONTHLY_REGISTRATIONS.csv
> The market share evolution shows the pincer's devastating effect. Monthly registrations: Ola went from **53,320 (Mar-24)** to **7,567 (Nov-25)**. TVS from 25,000 to **42,000**. Bajaj from 20,000 to **35,000**. Ather from 18,000 to **28,000**. Hero from 3,000 to **20,000**. The crossover where legacy first exceeded Ola: **Feb-24** (legacy 42.51% vs Ola 41.92%). The TROUGH for Ola's market share was **8.6% (Oct-25)** — they are now the #4 or #5 player.

### Competitive Disadvantages for Ola
1. **No established dealer network** — [[D2C]] is novel but unproven at scale vs incumbents' 4,000-7,000+ dealers
2. **[[Brand trust]] deficit** — Quality issues erode confidence vs decades-old brands ([[Market/Net_Promoter_Score|NPS]] negative vs Bajaj 40-50)
3. **Service network gap** — ~300 repair-capable centers vs thousands of incumbent service points
4. **No [[ICE]] revenue base** — Incumbents can subsidize EV transition with ICE profits (Bajaj ~Rs 15,000 Cr cash = 15+ years of EV subsidization)

### Competitive Advantages for Ola
1. **[[First-mover]] in pure EV** — Was 55.81% share (Apr-22), now 15.95% — advantage largely squandered
2. **Largest company-owned [[EV]] network** — 935 experience centres (but only ~300 with repair)
3. **[[Vertical integration]]** — [[Strategy/Backward Integration|In-house cell manufacturing]] delivers real cost leadership: battery cost $130 → $85/kWh
4. **[[Opportunities/PLI Dual Beneficiary]]** — Only EV maker with both Auto + Cell PLI
5. **Purpose-built [[EV platform]]** — [[Technology/Gen 2 Platform]] not compromised by ICE legacy, 68.85% component commonality

> | Repair centers | ~300 of 900 | 1,000+ | 1,480+ |
> | Turnaround | 7-15 days | 1-2 days | 1-2 days |
> | Parts availability | ~60-75% | 95% | 95% |
> | Service cost/visit | Rs 800-1,500 | Rs 500-800 | Rs 400 |

> 
> [[Companies/Bajaj Auto]]'s Rs 400 service cost is **2x to 3.75x cheaper** than Ola. Over 5 years with 12 visits/year, this saves Rs 24,000-66,000 — enough to offset any purchase price difference.
