# Scooter Breakdowns & Fires

> [!insight] The Single Most Important Thing
> Hardware reliability issues, including a high-profile battery fire, severely damaged consumer trust and triggered regulatory scrutiny, acting as a catalyst for the overall [[Service Crisis]]. The severity of the breakdowns quickly overwhelmed the service infrastructure.

> [!warning] Critical Incident: The Pune Fire
> On March 26, 2022, an Ola S1 Pro caught fire in Pune, Maharashtra. The Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MVL Section) issued a show-cause notice stating the cells failed the nail penetration test conducted by the Centre for Fire, Explosive and Environment Safety. Ola ultimately paid a penalty of ₹1,500,000.

> [!data] Source: risk_factors.txt
> "Lithium-ion cells have been known to occasionally release energy by venting smoke and flames... For instance, on March 26, 2022, one of our Ola S1 Pro scooters caught on fire in Pune." 
> The DRHP further disclosed that such incidents could subject the company to lawsuits, product recalls, or redesign efforts, and directly harm the brand's reputation.

> [!data] Source: CUSTOMER_SENTIMENT.csv
> Hardware failures and breakdowns resulted in a massive influx of complaints, overwhelming the service network and leading to a "Very Negative" Net Promoter Score. The virality of these breakdowns on [[Twitter]] and [[YouTube]] exponentially amplified the PR damage.
