> Ola's product quality issues are not limited to early production teething — they are a **growing liability** evidenced by warranty provisions that increased **5.3x in 18 months** (Rs 12.85 Cr FY22 to Rs 68.16 Cr Q1 FY24). The warranty escalation signals product failure rates are accelerating as more vehicles age in the field. Together with **500+ [[Risks/Consumer Court Crisis]] cases** (from 189 pre-[[IPO]]) and the [[Risks/CCPA_Regulatory_Intervention]] investigation, quality failures created the demand for service that the [[Risks/Service_Center_Crisis]] could not handle — a self-reinforcing negative cycle.

## Downside Scenario

A major product recall or safety incident could:
1. Destroy brand trust permanently in a market where reputation matters
2. Trigger large warranty expenses beyond provisions (already growing 5.3x in 18 months)
3. Lead to regulatory action ([[ARAI]] certification issues)
4. Create a negative flywheel: bad reputation → lower sales → less cash for quality improvement

> [!data] Source: SERVICE_NETWORK_COMPARISON.csv
> The quality crisis is amplified by the service network gap. With only **~300 repair-capable centers** vs TVS's **1,000+** and Bajaj's **1,480+**, and parts availability at **60-75%** vs legacy's **95%**, even minor defects become multi-week ordeals. [[Market/Google Play Store Rating]] rating of **3.2/5.0** (vs TVS 4.2, Ather 4.0) shows how quality problems are amplified through app reviews visible to every prospective buyer.
