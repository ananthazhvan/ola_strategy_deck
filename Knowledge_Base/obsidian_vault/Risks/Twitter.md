# Twitter / Social Media Reactions

> [!insight] The Single Most Important Thing
> Social media (specifically Twitter/X and YouTube) served as the leading indicator of Ola's [[Service Crisis]] long before it impacted financial statements. It created a feedback loop where viral complaint threads directly dampened prospective buyer trust, accelerating the [[Market Share Collapse]].

> [!data] Source: CUSTOMER_SENTIMENT.csv
> In 2025-2026, social media sentiment on Twitter/X turned predominantly negative. Viral complaint threads and the hashtag `#OlaElectric` were trending negatively due to service backlog and quality issues.

> [!data] Source: SERVICE_CRISIS_TIMELINE.csv
> By Q3-FY24, complaints began accelerating on Twitter and YouTube with viral negative reviews. Around 2,000 complaints were registered early on, before escalating to the CCPA level. 

> [!warning] Market Impact
> When tweets depicting severe issues (such as [[Scooter breakdowns]] or safety incidents) go viral, they often result in an immediate media cycle that correlates with pressure on the company's [[IPO]] valuation or stock price, forcing leadership to respond rapidly (e.g., launching Hyperservice).
