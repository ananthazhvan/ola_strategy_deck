## Slide 5: The Gigafactory — Asset or Stranded Risk?

**Central claim:** Ola's ₹4,000+ Cr Gigafactory is simultaneously its biggest advantage and its biggest vulnerability. The data tells both stories.

**Key data points:**

**The Build-Up (impressive execution):**

- Battery cost: $130/kWh (imported) → $85/kWh (in-house NMC+LFP) — **35% reduction**
- BOM reduction: 0% → **28%** achieved, 40% target by Mar 2028
- Ola is the only manufacturer qualifying for **both** PLI schemes (Automobile PLI + Cell PLI)
- Awarded 20 GWh under Cell PLI — highest allocation to any recipient

**The Risk (underutilization and cost parity):**
- Futurefactory installed capacity: 1M units/year → **16.4% utilization (FY26, 164K units sold)**
- Gigafactory at 6 GWh can supply ~1M scooters/year → Ola sold 164K → **massive excess capacity**
- Current in-house cost $85/kWh vs global LFP pack price **$80/kWh** — Ola is still ~6% ABOVE import parity
- Gigafactory only becomes economically justified at Phase 2: **12 GWh, $75/kWh (Jul 2027)**
- Global cell overcapacity: 4,200 GWh capacity vs ~3,300 GWh demand = **~900 GWh excess** — Chinese dumping suppresses prices
- Cell PLI requires: 1→5→10→20 GWh milestones; failure = TWICE the shortfall deducted from subsidy + possible discontinuation

**The LFP Pivot (right chemistry, right timing):**
- Cobalt spiked from $24,000/MT (H2 2025) → **$56,000/MT (current)** — 133% increase, DRC supply crisis
- LFP 46100 announced Apr 2026: $80/kWh, 3,000+ cycles (vs 1,500 NMC), better thermal stability for 45°C+ Indian summers
- LFP pack prices dropped from $140/kWh (2022) → **$80/kWh (2026)** — 43% decline, faster than NMC's 37.5%
- The cobalt spike + LFP price decline vindicates Ola's chemistry pivot

## Slide 7: Service Recovery — The Hub & Spoke Model

**Central claim:** The root cause of Ola's crisis wasn't product quality — it was service density. The fix isn't more Ola-owned centers. It's a franchise model.

**How It Works:**

- **300 Ola Hubs** — company-owned, located in Tier 1/2 cities. Handle complex repairs (battery, motor, frame), warranty claims, and refurbishment. Staffed by Ola-certified engineers. Stock full parts inventory.
- **2,000 Certified Local Garages** — existing independent mechanics in Tier 2/3/4 towns. Ola provides: certification training (2 weeks), diagnostic tools (Ola AI app), priority parts supply (48-hour delivery), branded signage. Garage provides: labor, local trust, existing customer relationships.
- **1,000 Hyperservice Vans** (already deployed) — mobile repair for common issues at customer's location. AI-powered remote diagnostics first, dispatch only if needed.

**The Economics:**
- Ola-owned center: **₹1.5 Cr** to build and operate → serves one location
- Hub & Spoke: **₹60 lakh/hub + ₹60,000/garage certification** → 2,300 service points for ₹300 Cr total
- **10x more service points per rupee** vs the old Ola-owned model
- Comparable to Bajaj's 1,480+ dealers at **20% of the construction cost**

**What This Fixes:**
- Coverage: 95% of customers within **15 km** of a service point (vs current situation where rural customers travel 50+ km)
- Parts availability: 60-75% → **95% target** (Ola hubs stock full inventory, garages get 48-hour priority delivery)
- Turnaround: 7-15 days → **1-2 days** (routine at certified garage, complex at hub)
- Trust: local mechanic you already know vs anonymous Ola center — **trust inheritance from the mechanic, not the brand**

**Competitive Parity Table:**
| Metric | Ola (Old) | Ola (Hub & Spoke Target) | Bajaj | TVS |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Service Points | ~300 | 2,300 | 1,480+ | 1,000+ |
| Parts Availability | 60-75% | 95% | 95% | 95% |
| Turnaround | 7-15 days | 1-2 days | 1-2 days | 1-2 days |
| Cost/Visit | ₹800-1,500 | ₹400-600 | ₹400 | ₹500-700 |
| Independent Mechanic Access | No | Yes | Yes | Yes |

**Visual element:** Diagram: 300 large circles (hubs) connected by lines to 2,000 smaller circles (garages), representing the hub-and-spoke topology.

## Slide 8: Marketing — Radical Transparency (₹80 Cr)

- Traditional advertising won't fix this — claims of "improved service" from a distrusted brand are dismissed as PR
- The only credible messenger is **the factory floor, the service dashboard, and other customers**

**Campaign Architecture (₹80 Cr, 12 months):**

**Pillar 1: Live Gigafactory Streams (₹20 Cr)**
- 24/7 live cameras on the Krishnagiri production line and Gigafactory cell line
- Public dashboard: cells produced today, defect rate, vehicles shipped, warranty claims received/resolved
- Message: "You can watch us build your scooter. If we mess up, you'll see it."
- Platform: YouTube live + embedded on olaelectric.com + in-app

**Pillar 2: Public Service Dashboard (₹15 Cr)**
- Real-time map: every service center, current wait time, parts stock level
- Live tracker: your scooter's repair status (like Domino's pizza tracker but for service)
- Aggregate metrics: avg turnaround time this week, parts availability %, complaint resolution rate
- If numbers are bad, they're bad publicly — the commitment to show bad numbers is the trust signal

**Pillar 3: Engineers, Not Influencers (₹25 Cr)**
- YouTube documentary series: "Inside the Gigafactory" — 8 episodes following the cell engineering team
- "The Service Garage" — 8 episodes from certified garage owners, showing real repairs, real customers
- Zero paid influencers, zero celebrity endorsements — the product engineers and garage mechanics are the faces
- Cost goes to production quality, not talent fees

**Pillar 4: The 48-Hour Promise (₹10 Cr operational)**
- If your scooter isn't fixed in 48 hours → loaner scooter delivered to you + ₹5,000 cash compensation

**Pillar 5: Earned Media + Community (₹10 Cr)**

- Regional owner meetups at Gigafactory — factory tour + Q&A with engineering team
- Press: proactive data releases (service metrics every quarter, independently audited)

**Channel Mix:**
- 35% YouTube (documentaries, live streams)
- 25% Owned platforms (app, website, email, community)
- 20% Earned media (press, community, word-of-mouth)
- 20% Local activation (garage-level branding, dealer events)
- **0% traditional/OOH, 0% performance ads** — paid media is the least credible channel for a brand in trust crisis

## Slide 9: Post-Subsidy Survival — The 3-Part Pricing Architecture

**Central claim:** When PM E-DRIVE ends Jul 31, 2026, every E2W loses ₹5,000/unit. For Ola at 4.5% EBITDA margin, that's 123% of per-unit profit. The math says Ola should die. Here's why it doesn't.

**The Cliff:**
- PM E-DRIVE ends **July 31, 2026** for E2W — ₹5,000/vehicle subsidy disappears
- Ola's EBITDA/unit at Q4 FY26: ~₹4,050 (₹90,000 ASP × 4.5% margin)
- ₹5,000 subsidy loss = **123% of per-unit profit wiped out**
- When FAME-II ended Mar 2024 (subsidy dropped ₹40,000→₹5,000), Ola's volume dropped **36% MoM** (53,320→33,963)
- Karnataka road tax policy also changed Apr 2026: ended 100% exemption, now 5-10% LTT — **adds ₹7,000-14,000 in Ola's home market**
- Gujarat subsidy discontinued 2025. Multiple states rationalizing EV incentives simultaneously.

**The 3-Part Defense:**

**Part 1: Cost Hardening — Save ₹3,000/unit**
| Source | Saving/unit | How |
|---|---|---|
| Gigafactory cell cost (LFP 46100, $80/kWh vs $130 import) | ₹1,800 | In-house cells for all production by Q2 FY26 |
| Gen 3 platform BOM reduction (common parts, lighter frame) | ₹800 | 68.85% component commonality across models |
| Logistics optimization (regional warehouse network) | ₹250 | 5 regional hubs instead of central dispatch |
| PLI dual disbursement (Auto + Cell PLI, pending claims) | ₹150 | ₹2,322 Cr disbursed so far, significant pending |

**Part 2: State Arbitrage — Recover ₹1,500/unit**

Shift sales and inventory allocation to high-subsidy states:
- **Delhi:** Up to ₹30,000/vehicle (highest state subsidy) + 100% road tax waiver
- **Maharashtra:** ₹10,000/vehicle + 100% road tax + registration exemption + toll waivers
- **Tamil Nadu:** ₹10,000-20,000/vehicle + 100% road tax exemption (Ola's factory state)
- Strategy: prioritize inventory, marketing spend, and service rollout in these 3 states
- These states = ~40% of India's E2W demand — concentrate where the math works

**Part 3: Product Mix Absorption — Absorb remaining ₹500/unit**
- **Ola Care subscription** (₹499/month): priority service, annual maintenance, extended warranty → incremental revenue without hardware cost
- Accessories attachment: helmets, phone mounts, chargers sold at point of purchase → ₹300-500/unit margin
- **S1 X holds at ₹69,999** (entry price point) — premium models (S1 Pro+, Gen 3) absorb the pass-through
- Price increase to consumer: **₹0 on S1 X, ₹1,500-2,500 on S1 Pro+/Gen 3** (price-insensitive premium buyers)

**Net Impact:**
- Subsidy loss: -₹5,000/unit
- Cost hardening: +₹3,000/unit
- State arbitrage: +₹1,500/unit
- Product mix: +₹500/unit
- **Net: ₹0 impact on per-unit EBITDA** — survival without price hikes on mass models

**Visual element:** Waterfall chart: start at current EBITDA/unit → -₹5,000 subsidy loss → +₹3,000 cost savings → +₹1,500 state recovery → +₹500 mix = same EBITDA.

**Transition hook:** Everything converges on one question: can Ola execute all of this in 12 months? Here's the checklist.
